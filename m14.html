MODULE 14·Medieval Bihar — Final

Sufism, Sikhism &
Tribal States in Bihar

While kings and battles dominated medieval Bihar's political landscape, three other forces shaped its soul: Sufi saints who preached love across communities, Sikh Gurus who chose Patna as their home, and tribal dynasties who governed Bihar's hinterlands for centuries.

BPSC Special Mains GS-I ⚠ Trap: Makhdoom Maneri born at Maner (Patna), 1264. Guru Gobind Singh born Patna 1666, NOT born at Patna Sahib — Patna Sahib is where he was born but the Gurdwara is named Patna Sahib.
🎭Story HookSTART HERE

The year is 1666 AD. Aurangzeb sits on the Mughal throne in Delhi, enforcing a strict version of Islam across India. In the same year, in the same empire, a boy is born in Patna whose entire life will be devoted to challenging religious oppression with spiritual courage. His name: Gobind Rai — later Guru Gobind Singh.

Twenty-four years earlier, in 1264 AD, another extraordinary man had been born in the village of Maner, near Patna. He would become the most revered Sufi saint in Bihar's history — Makhdoom Sharfuddin Ahmad Yahya Maneri — whose teachings of love, humility, and mystical devotion drew both Muslims and Hindus to his dargah at Bihar Sharif.

These two men — one who preached in Arabic and Persian, one who preached in Punjabi — both chose Bihar as their spiritual homeland. Between them, and the tribal kings of Palamu and Bhojpur, they tell the complete human story of medieval Bihar: not just conquest and administration, but faith, song, and resistance.

💡 Patna Sahib — where Guru Gobind Singh was born — is today the second most important Sikh pilgrimage site after the Golden Temple, Amritsar. Bihar gave Sikhism its most celebrated birthplace.

Sufism in Bihar
1264 AD – Medieval Period
School Bihar's Sufism belonged to the Wajudia School. Reached Bihar even before Turkish conquest. Early orders: Chisti and Suharawardi.
Orders in Bihar Bihar had multiple Sufi orders: Emam Taj Fakeeh, Chisti, Kadri, Suharvardi, Firdausi, Nakshbandi. Bihar Sharif and Saran = notable centres of Chisti Sufism.
Makhdoom Maneri ⭐ Makhdoom Sharfuddin Ahmad Yahya Maneri — most prominent Sufi saint of Bihar. Born: Maner village, Patna district, July 1264 AD. Left at age 12 for Arabic, Persian, logic, philosophy studies. Given title "Firdausi" by Sheikh Najeebuddin Firdausi. Wrote: Maktubat-i-Sadi, Maktubati-Bist-o-Hasht, Fawaed-i-Rukni. Tomb: Badi Dargah, Bihar Sharif (Nalanda district) — still major pilgrimage site.
Sufi Literature 3 types: Tajkira (life of saints) · Malkujat (dialogues between saints) · Maktubaat (writings of saints). Sharfuddin Yahya Maneri's Malfuzat and Maktubat = key historical sources for Tughlaq-era Bihar.
Other Saints Maulana Muzaffar Balkhi, Hazrat Latifuddin, Hazrat Fazaullah prominent Sufi saints. Abul Faiz Qazin Ola of Bania Basarh (Vaishali) = greatest Sufi of Shuttasia order. Maner (Patna) = major Sufi dargah even today.
🙏
Sikhism in Bihar
1509 AD – 1666 AD · BPSC Special
Guru Nanak Guru Nanak Dev visited Patna in 1509 AD. Stayed at Bhagat Jaitmal's house near Gaighat, Patna. Also visited Gaya, Rajgir, Munger, Bhagalpur, Kahalgaon. Propagated Sikhism; made followers in these regions.
Guru Teg Bahadur Guru Teg Bahadur came to Patna with family in 1666 AD on his way to Assam on a peace mission. Left his wife at Patna → she gave birth here. Guru Teg Bahadur was executed in Delhi by Aurangzeb in 1675 AD.
Guru Gobind Singh ⭐ 10th and last Sikh Guru. Born at Patna Sahib, Patna in 1666 AD. Installed as Guru at age 9 after father Guru Teg Bahadur was beheaded. Spiritual leader, poet AND warrior. Prepared Sikh warrior class called Khalsa (established Baishak 1699). Finalised Guru Granth Sahib from Kartarpur Pothi.
Patna Sahib ⭐ Takht Shri Harmandir Sahib (also known as Patna Sahib) = one of the five Takhts of Sikhism. Second most important Sikh pilgrimage site after Golden Temple, Amritsar. The house where Guru Gobind Singh was born is a place of pilgrimage.
Sikh Gurudwaras Famous Gurudwaras in Patna: Gurdwara Ghai Ghat, Gurdwara Gobind Ghat, Gurdwara Guru Ka Bagh, Gurdwara Bal Leela, Gurdwara Handi Saheb. After Partition, many Sikhs came to Patna — most are Nanak Panthi.

Tribal States — Chero & Ujjaini
Medieval Period · Bihar's Independent Kingdoms
Chero Dynasty Established strong state in Shahabad, Saran, Champaran, Muzaffarpur, Palamu. Ruled for ~300 years. Had four estates in Shahabad district. Most famous ruler: Medini Rai — territory extended to Gaya, Dawoodnagar, Arwal, Ramgarh. Jagdishpur fair started by Fulchand (Chero-era tradition). 1587–1607: Kukumchand Jharap = head of Bhojpur Cheros. Eventually assimilated into Mughal Empire.
Chero vs Ujjaini 1611 AD — decisive battle between Ujjainis and Cheros. Narayan Mal = Ujjaini king. Ujjainis won. Three Mughal invasions during Pratap Rai's (Medini Rai's son) rule.
Ujjaini Dynasty Parmer Rajputs originally from Ujjain → settled in Bihar → called Ujjainiya. Bhojraj (king) fled Alauddin Khalji's conquest of Dhar (Malwa) with son Devraj. Took shelter under Chero King Raja Mukund. Devraj killed Chero king Sahasbal (1324) → founded city of Bhojpur. Ujjainiyas were powerful in Buxar, Jagdishpur, Dumraon — remained till British era. Gajpat = Ujjaini ruler who allied with Sher Shah Suri and defeated Bengal Sultanate together.

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2015
Which Sikh Guru was born in Patna?
(a) Guru Nanak
(b) Guru Teg Bahadur
(c) Guru Hargobind
(d) Guru Gobind Singh
✓ CORRECT: (d) Guru Gobind Singh

Guru Gobind Singh, the 10th and last Sikh Guru, was born at Patna Sahib, Patna in 1666 AD. His father Guru Teg Bahadur had left his family at Patna while on a peace mission to Assam. Guru Nanak visited Patna (1509) but was not born there. Guru Teg Bahadur passed through Patna but was also not born there. Guru Hargobind (6th Guru) has no specific Bihar connection.

BPSC PrelimsMCQ Bank
Jagdishpur fair in Bihar was started during which dynasty?
(a) Noohani dynasty
(b) Chero dynasty
(c) Ujjaini dynasty
(d) Sur dynasty
✓ CORRECT: (b) Chero dynasty

The Jagdishpur fair was started by Fulchand during the Chero dynasty. The Chero dynasty established their state in Bhojpur, Shahabad, Saran, Champaran, Muzaffarpur and Palamu and ruled for about 300 years. The fair at Jagdishpur became a notable cultural tradition of the region during this period.

BPSC PrelimsMCQ Bank
Buxar was the capital of which Bihar tribal dynasty?
(a) Ujjaini
(b) Chero
(c) Noohani
(d) Sur
✓ CORRECT: (a) Ujjaini

The Ujjaini (Ujjainiya) dynasty was powerful in Buxar, Jagdishpur and Dumraon. They were Parmer Rajputs originally from Ujjain who settled in west Bihar (Bhojpur/Shahabad region). They founded Bhojpur city (Devraj, 1324 AD) and remained influential until the British arrived. Chero territory was Muzaffarpur, Saran, Champaran area — not Buxar specifically.

✅Concept CheckTEST YOURSELF
1. Makhdoom Sharfuddin Ahmad Yahya Maneri was the most prominent Sufi saint of Bihar. His tomb (dargah) is at which place?
✓ Correct! Makhdoom Maneri was born at Maner (Patna) but his tomb — the Badi Dargah — is at Bihar Sharif, Nalanda district. He is called "Makhdoom" (one who is served/respected). His dargah at Bihar Sharif is still a major pilgrimage site for both Muslims and Hindus.
✗ Answer is (B) Bihar Sharif, Nalanda. He was BORN at Maner (Patna) but his TOMB is at Badi Dargah, Bihar Sharif (Nalanda district). Birth location ≠ Tomb location. This distinction is a common BPSC trap.
2. Takht Shri Harmandir Sahib (Patna Sahib) is one of the five Takhts of Sikhism. It marks the birthplace of which Sikh Guru?
✓ Correct! Takht Shri Harmandir Sahib (Patna Sahib) marks the birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh — the 10th Sikh Guru — born here in 1666 AD. It is the second most important Sikh pilgrimage site after the Golden Temple, Amritsar, and one of the five Takhts (seats of Sikh temporal authority).
✗ Answer is (C) Guru Gobind Singh. He was born at Patna Sahib in 1666 AD. Guru Nanak visited Patna (1509) but was born elsewhere. Guru Teg Bahadur passed through Patna but was also born elsewhere.
MAINS GS-I · 150 WORDS
Bihar has been a significant centre for both Sufi and Sikh traditions. Discuss the contributions of Makhdoom Maneri and Guru Gobind Singh to Bihar's spiritual heritage.
Introduction

Medieval Bihar produced two of India's most influential religious figures — the Sufi saint Makhdoom Maneri (1264 AD) and the Sikh Guru Gobind Singh (1666 AD). Both were shaped by Bihar, and both left legacies that transcend medieval times.

Makhdoom Maneri's Contributions
  • Born at Maner (Patna) in 1264 AD; leader of the Firdausi Sufi order in Bihar
  • Preached love, humility, and mystical devotion — attracting both Muslim and Hindu followers
  • His writings (Maktubat-i-Sadi, Maktubati-Bist-o-Hasht) are invaluable historical sources for Tughlaq-era Bihar
  • His Badi Dargah at Bihar Sharif remains a Hindu-Muslim shared pilgrimage site — a living symbol of composite culture
Guru Gobind Singh's Bihar Connection
  • Born at Patna Sahib in 1666 AD — a Bihari birthplace for the Sikh tradition's most celebrated warrior-saint
  • Established the Khalsa (1699) — transforming Sikhism from a devotional to a warrior-spiritual tradition
  • Patna Sahib (Takht Shri Harmandir Sahib) = 2nd holiest Sikh site; brings millions of pilgrims to Bihar annually
Conclusion

Bihar's spiritual diversity — Buddhist, Jain, Hindu, Sufi, Sikh — makes it one of India's most religiously significant regions. Maneri and Gobind Singh represent this pluralism: a Sufi saint and a Sikh Guru, born four centuries apart on the same soil, both preaching the primacy of love and courage over oppression.
