MODULE 02·Ancient Bihar

Mahajanapadas —
Anga, Vajji & Magadha

By 600 BC, power in northern India was consolidating into 16 great kingdoms. Three of them — Anga, Vajji, and Magadha — were in Bihar. One of them would become the world's first republic. Another would become the world's mightiest empire.

BPSC Special Mains GS-I ~600 BC – 400 BC ⚠ Trap: Vajji ≠ Licchavi (Vajji is the confederacy; Licchavi is a clan within it)
🎭 Story Hook START HERE

The year is 500 BC. In a magnificent assembly hall on the north bank of the Ganga, 7,707 elected representatives are debating the fate of their republic. They vote. They decide. No king speaks for them — the people speak for themselves.

This is Vaishali — a city in what is now Muzaffarpur district of Bihar — and it is making history that most modern democracies don't know about. While Greece was building its Athenian democracy, Bihar already had one. The Licchavi republic of Vaishali is considered the world's first republic — 2,500 years before the United Nations existed.

A few hundred kilometres south, in the rocky hills around Rajgir, another state was quietly building something different — not a republic, but a military machine. Magadha was learning to use iron, elephants, and strategic marriages to swallow its neighbours whole. What started as one of 16 Mahajanapadas would, within a century, become the foundation of India's first empire.

💡 Bihar at 600 BC was where both Democracy AND Empire were being invented — simultaneously, just 200 km apart.
🧠 Core Concept — The 16 + 3 WITH ANALOGY
🌍 Modern Analogy

Think of Mahajanapadas like 16 startup companies competing in the same market (the Gangetic plains). Some were monarchies (CEOs with full power), some were republics (board-governed). Eventually, four giants dominated: Vatsa, Avanti, Kosala, and Magadha — like four big tech companies absorbing all the others. Bihar had 3 of these 16 startups — and two of them (Vajji and Magadha) became the most influential of all.

🏛️ Anga
Capital: CHAMPA (Bhagalpur)
NE of Magadha. Comprised present Bhagalpur, Munger, Khagaria. First mentioned in Atharvaveda. Kingdom of Karna (Mahabharata). Conquered by Bimbisara.
Type: Monarchy
🏛️ Vajji (Vriji)
Capital: VAISHALI
8-9 confederated clans. Licchavis most powerful. Spread over Champaran, Saran, Muzaffarpur, Darbhanga. World's first republic. Mahavira born here.
Type: Republic (Gana-Sangha)
🏛️ Magadha
Capital: RAJGIR → Pataliputra
South of Ganga, Patna-Gaya region. Became superpower under Haryanka, Nanda, Maurya dynasties. Birthplace of Buddhism & Jainism.
Type: Monarchy → Empire
⏳ Timeline — Rise & Fall of the Three CHRONOLOGICAL
~600 BC
16 Mahajanapadas Formed From Janapadas
Later Vedic period small kingdoms (Janapadas) combined into 16 larger entities. Three in Bihar: Anga, Vajji, Magadha. Sources: Anguttara Nikaya lists all 16; Jain texts corroborate.
~600 BC — Vajji Republic at PEAK
Vaishali — World's First Republic
Licchavi Republic with 7,707-member assembly. Capital at Vaishali (modern Basarh village, Muzaffarpur). Aamrapali was royal courtesan (Nagar-Vadhu). Mahavira born at Kundagram in Vaishali. Buddha delivered last sermon here.
~544 BC
Bimbisara Founds Haryanka Dynasty Anga Absorbed
Bimbisara kills last Anga king Brahmadatta — Anga merged into Magadha permanently. Magadha's rise begins. Champa (Bhagalpur) becomes Magadha province under Ajatashatru as viceroy.
~492–460 BC
Ajatashatru Destroys Vajji Republic
Ajatashatru used his minister Vassakara to sow dissension among Licchavi leaders, then attacked with new weapons: Rathamusala (chariot with mace) and Mahasilakantaka (catapult). War lasted 16 years. Vajji finally absorbed into Magadha.
~500–400 BC
16 → 4 Mahajanapadas Remain
By 400 BC, only 4 major powers left: Vatsa, Avanti, Kosala, Magadha. Magadha had already absorbed Anga and Vajji. The stage was set for the Nanda and then Maurya empires.
🔄 Flowchart — How Magadha Became Supreme CAUSE → EVENT → RESULT
Causes — Why Magadha Won

Geographic + Resource Advantage

Richest iron deposits near Rajgir. Fertile Gangetic plains. Protected by hills on south. River highways for trade and military movement.

↓
Tool 1

💍 Marriage Alliances

Bimbisara married: Kosaladevi (got Kashi as dowry), Chellana (Licchavi), Kshema (Madra). Diplomatic expansion without war.

Tool 2

⚔️ Military Innovation

First permanent army. Elephant corps (9,000 under Chandragupta). New weapons: Rathamusala, Mahasilakantaka. Iron weapons from local mines.

Tool 3

🏙️ Strategic Capital Shift

Rajgir → Pataliputra (455 BC) by Udayin. River confluence gave trade control. Later became the greatest city of ancient India.

↓
Result

Magadha Dominates All of India by 321 BC

Foundation for Mauryan Empire. Chandragupta Maurya builds on Magadha's power to create first pan-Indian empire. Magadha's history became India's history.

👑 Special: Videha (Mithila) — The Forgotten Kingdom BPSC SPECIAL

📍 Location & Origin

North of Ganga; N Bihar + eastern Terai of Nepal. First mentioned in Yajurveda (not Rigveda). Founded by Ikshvaku's son Nimi Videh. His descendant Mithi Janaka founded city of Mithila. All later kings called "Janaka."

🌟 Famous Connections

Sita was princess of Videha — daughter of Raja Janaka. Capital was Janakpur (now in Nepal). According to Brihadaranyaka Upanishad, Raja Janaka organised a philosophical debate won by Yajnavalkya. Last king: Karal Janaka.

⚠️ Why Not in 16 Mahajanapadas?

Videha had already declined before the Mahajanapada era consolidated. By 6th century BC, Videha territory merged into the Vajji confederacy. This is why it's not in Anguttara Nikaya's list of 16 — even though it was once a great kingdom.

📜 Sources

Mentioned in Ramayana (Sita), Mahabharata (Janaka), Brihadaranyaka Upanishad (philosopher-king Janaka), and Yajurveda (first mention). Satapatha Brahmana describes Aryan expansion into Videha region.

📝 PYQ Practice SOLVED
BPSC Prelims BPSC 2008
Champa was the capital of which Mahajanapada — known today as which district?
(a) Bhagalpur
(b) Khagaria
(c) Munger
(d) Patliputra
✓ CORRECT ANSWER: (a) Bhagalpur

Champa was the capital of Anga Mahajanapada, corresponding to present-day Bhagalpur. Earlier name was Malini; renamed Champa after a king. Hiuen Tsang called it "Chenpo." First king of Anga was Titikshu. Karna of Mahabharata was king of Anga. The kingdom included Bhagalpur, Munger, and Khagaria districts.

BPSC Prelims BPSC 2011
'Amrapali' the famous dancer was related to which city?
(a) Patliputra
(b) Vaishali
(c) Mithila
(d) Munger
✓ CORRECT ANSWER: (b) Vaishali

Aamrapali was the royal courtesan and dancer of Vaishali. She was called "Nagar-Vadhu" (bride of the city) of Vaishali. She later became a devout follower of Buddha. She is mentioned in Buddhist texts and her story represents the cultural richness of the Licchavi republic. She offered her mango grove to Buddha — a famous story in Buddhist literature.

BPSC Prelims BPSC 2011
What was the early capital of Magadha?
(a) Patliputra
(b) Vaishali
(c) Rajgriha (Girivraja)
(d) Champa
✓ CORRECT ANSWER: (c) Rajgriha (Girivraja)

⚠️ Trap: Many confuse Pataliputra as the original capital — but Pataliputra was founded LATER by Udayin in 455 BC. The EARLY capital of Magadha was Rajgriha (also called Girivraja — "city of five hills"). Rajgir was the capital during Brihadratha, Haryanka (Bimbisara, Ajatashatru), and Shishunaga dynasties. Pataliputra became capital only after Udayin shifted it.

MAINS GS-I 150 words
Vaishali is described as the world's first republic. Discuss the democratic features of the Vajji confederacy and examine why its republican system ultimately failed against Magadha's monarchical aggression.
Introduction

Vaishali, capital of the Licchavi clan within the Vajji confederacy, operated as a republic (Gana-Sangha) around 600 BC — predating Greek democracy by over a century.

Democratic Features
  • Elected Assembly: Central legislature of 7,707 members (Rajas), each called a Raja
  • Assembly Hall: Santhagara — where debates and decisions were held collectively
  • Deliberative System: Decisions made by consensus, not royal decree
  • Clan-based Governance: Ugras, Bhogas, Jnatrikas, Licchavis all had representation
Why it Failed vs. Magadha
  • Internal divisions: Ajatashatru's minister Vassakara sowed dissension among Licchavi leaders over 3 years
  • Military asymmetry: Magadha had permanent army, new weapons (Rathamusala, Mahasilakantaka); Vajji relied on part-time militia
  • Slow decision-making: Republican deliberation could not match monarchical speed in military response
Conclusion

The Vajji Republic demonstrates that democratic governance existed in ancient India. Its fall reveals the paradox that participatory systems, while socially superior, may be militarily vulnerable to focused autocratic power — a lesson that echoes through history.
