MODULE 20·Modern Bihar

Eminent Freedom Fighters
of Bihar

Bihar produced India's first President, its most enduring Prime Ministers, its first tribal political icon, its greatest Lok Nayak, and a revolutionary who killed a government informer to avenge Bhagat Singh. This is Bihar's hall of fame for the freedom struggle.

BPSC Special Mains GS-I ⚠ Key traps: Rajendra Prasad born Ziradei (Siwan). JP born Sitab Diyara (Saran). Shri Krishna Singh born Nawada. Baikuntha Shukla hanged Gaya jail 14 May 1934.
🎭Story Hook

The year is 9 December 1946. The Constituent Assembly of India meets for its very first session. The man presiding over this historic gathering — the person who will oversee the drafting of the world's longest written constitution — is a 75-year-old man from a small village called Ziradei in Siwan district, Bihar.

His name: Dr Rajendra Prasad. Son of a farmer, a child prodigy who mastered Sanskrit at 12, first-class lawyer, Gandhi's most devoted lieutenant in Bihar, and now — the man who will become India's first President.

Bihar gave India its first Head of State. Bihar also gave India its most daring revolutionary (Jayaprakash Narayan), its first tribal political martyr (Birsa Munda), its most fearless Dalit leader (Jagjivan Ram), and a young man who avenged Bhagat Singh's hanging by killing the government informer — Baikuntha Shukla, hanged in Gaya Jail on 14 May 1934.

💡 Of India's 14 Presidents till 2017, only one has won the Bharat Ratna TWICE — Dr Rajendra Prasad, Bihar's own son. He remains the only person to serve as India's President for two full terms.

📝PYQ Practice
BPSC MCQ
Jayaprakash Narayan (JP) was posthumously awarded the Bharat Ratna in:
(a) 1990
(b) 1995
(c) 1999
(d) 2002
✓ CORRECT: (c) 1999

Jayaprakash Narayan (Lok Nayak, JP) was posthumously awarded the Bharat Ratna in 1999. He had died on 8 October 1979. JP was born on 11 October 1902 at Sitab Diyara, Saran district. He also received the Magsaysay Award for Public Service in 1965. The Patna International Airport is named after him.

BPSC MCQ
Baikuntha Shukla was hanged in which jail for killing Phanindra Nath Ghosh?
(a) Patna Central Jail
(b) Gaya Central Jail
(c) Lahore Jail
(d) Muzaffarpur Jail
✓ CORRECT: (b) Gaya Central Jail

Baikuntha Shukla was hanged at Gaya Central Jail on 14 May 1934. He had killed Phanindra Nath Ghosh, the government informer whose testimony led to the hanging of Bhagat Singh, Sukhdev, and Rajguru in 1931. Baikuntha Shukla was a member of the HSRA (Hindustan Socialist Republican Association) and had participated in the Salt Satyagraha of 1930.

BPSC MCQ
Dr Rajendra Prasad was born on 3rd December 1884 in which district of Bihar?
(a) Saran
(b) Muzaffarpur
(c) Siwan
(d) Champaran
✓ CORRECT: (c) Siwan

Rajendra Prasad was born on 3 December 1884 in village Ziradei, Siwan district, Bihar. He became India's first President in 1950 and served two full terms. He was elected President of the Constituent Assembly on 11 December 1946. He died on 28 February 1963 in Patna. He received the Bharat Ratna in 1962.

✅Concept Check
1. Jayaprakash Narayan was born at which place?
✓ Correct! JP Narayan was born at Sitab Diyara, Saran district on 11 October 1902. Don't confuse: Ziradei (Siwan) = Rajendra Prasad; Nawada = Shri Krishna Singh; Chandwa (Bhojpur) = Jagjivan Ram. All four are standard BPSC questions.
✗ Answer is (B) Sitab Diyara, Saran. JP Narayan's birthplace. Ziradei (Siwan) = Rajendra Prasad. Nawada = Shri Krishna Singh. Chandwa (Bhojpur) = Jagjivan Ram.
2. Who was the first person to be appointed Interim President of the Constituent Assembly of India (9 December 1946)?
✓ Correct! Sachchidananda Sinha (born Arrah, Bhojpur) was named as the Interim President of the Constituent Assembly on 9 December 1946 — the day of its first meeting. Rajendra Prasad was elected as the permanent President of the Constituent Assembly on 11 December 1946. The distinction between "interim" (Sachchidananda Sinha) and "elected" (Rajendra Prasad) is frequently tested.
✗ Answer is (B) Sachchidananda Sinha. He was the INTERIM (temporary, first day) president of the Constituent Assembly on 9 Dec 1946. Rajendra Prasad was ELECTED as permanent President on 11 Dec 1946. This is the most commonly confused Bihar Constituent Assembly fact.

Bihar has been called "the cradle of India's freedom movement." Justify this statement by describing the contributions of at least THREE eminent freedom fighters from Bihar.
Introduction

Bihar's contribution to India's freedom struggle was disproportionate to its size — producing India's first President, its most daring revolutionary, its most devoted Gandhian, and fighters who went from Bihar's fields to Andaman's Kalapani.

Dr Rajendra Prasad (1884–1963)
  • Joined Champaran Satyagraha (1917) as Gandhi's lieutenant — became Gandhi's most trusted Bihar associate
  • Led Bihar's Non-Cooperation Movement (1920); imprisoned during Salt Satyagraha (1930) and Quit India (1942)
  • Elected President of Constituent Assembly (11 Dec 1946) — oversaw drafting of India's Constitution
  • Became India's first President (1950) — serving two full terms (the only President to do so)
Jayaprakash Narayan (1902–1979)
  • Founded Congress Socialist Party (1934) — gave Bihar and India a socialist political tradition
  • Led Quit India in Bihar (1942) when all senior leaders were arrested; organised Azad Dasta (1943)
  • Later led JP Movement (1974) against Emergency — the only political figure to challenge Indira Gandhi successfully
  • Bharat Ratna (1999, posthumous); Patna airport named after him
Baikuntha Shukla (1907–1934)

A young revolutionary who killed Phanindra Nath Ghosh (the informer who caused Bhagat Singh's hanging) and willingly accepted death at Gaya Central Jail (14 May 1934) — a reminder that Bihar's freedom contribution was not only political but also sacrificial.

Conclusion

From Champaran's fields (Rajendra Prasad), to Quit India's underground (JP), to Gaya's jail (Baikuntha Shukla) — Bihar's freedom fighters represent every mode of resistance India employed against colonial rule. The state earned its title as India's freedom cradle not through one great leader but through generations of sacrifice.
