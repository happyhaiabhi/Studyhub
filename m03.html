MODULE 03·Ancient Bihar

Pre-Mauryan Dynasties
of Magadha

Four dynasties. Two centuries. An empire in the making. From the first king Brihadratha to the last Nanda who faced Alexander's shadow — this is how Magadha became the foundation of India's first great empire.

BPSC Prelims Mains GS-I 544 BC – 321 BC ⚠ Trap: Ajatashatru = Haryanka dynasty (NOT Maurya); Pataliputra founded 455 BC by Udayin (NOT Ajatashatru)
🎭Story HookSTART HERE

The year is 321 BC. Alexander the Great has turned back from India. In Pataliputra, the richest city in the world, the last Nanda king Dhana Nanda sleeps peacefully. His treasury is legendary. His army — 200,000 infantry, 80,000 cavalry, 6,000 elephants — is the most feared in Asia.

But in the shadows, a young man from uncertain origins — some say Shudra-born, others say Kshatriya — is meeting secretly with a sharp-tongued professor named Chanakya. Chanakya has a grudge. Dhana Nanda once humiliated him in court, pulled him by his topknot, and threw him out. Chanakya swore then that he would never tie his topknot again until he had destroyed the Nanda dynasty.

The young man is Chandragupta Maurya. And the dynasty about to be destroyed — the Nandas — is the final chapter in a fascinating story that began 200 years earlier with a warrior named Bimbisara and a kingdom called Magadha.

💡 Fun Fact: Chanakya's untied topknot is one of history's most dramatic political vows — and it ended one of India's most powerful dynasties.
🧠Core Concept — Four Dynasties, One Empire BuiltWITH ANALOGY
🏗️ Modern Analogy

Think of Magadha's rise like building a skyscraper. Brihadratha dynasty = laying the foundation (established Magadha as a kingdom). Haryanka = building the first 10 floors (Bimbisara's alliances + Ajatashatru's conquests). Shishunaga = reinforcing the structure (defeated Avanti's 100-year rivalry). Nanda = building the top floors and filling the treasury. Maurya (next module) = adding the spire and calling it the world's greatest building.

~6th CENTURY BC (MYTHOLOGICAL ERA)
1. Brihadratha Dynasty
EARLIEST
Brihadratha Founder Eldest son of Vasu (Kuru king of Chedi). Founded Magadha; established capital at Girivraja (Rajgir). Mentioned in Rigveda.
Jarasandha Most famous Son of Brihadratha. Extended empire to Chedi, Anga, Pundra, Vanga, Kalinga. Killed by Bhima in Mahabharata (with Krishna's help). Capital: Rajgir.
Ripunjaya Last ruler Killed by his minister Pulika, who installed his own son. According to Puranas, Pradyota dynasty followed briefly before Haryanka.
544 BC – 412 BC
2. Haryanka Dynasty
⭐ MOST TESTED
Bimbisara 544–492 BC Founder of Haryanka dynasty. Capital: Rajgir. Married at age 15 (per Mahavamsa). Contemporary of Buddha. Policy of 3 marriage alliances: Kosaladevi (got Kashi dowry), Chellana (Licchavi), Kshema (Madra/Punjab). Known as Shrenika in Jain texts. First ruler to form permanent army. Sent physician Jivaka to Avanti king.
Ajatashatru 492–460 BC Imprisoned and killed his father Bimbisara. Childhood name: Kunika. Conquered Vaishali (16-year war; used minister Vassakara + new weapons). Both Buddha (487 BC) and Mahavira (468 BC) died in his reign. 1st Buddhist Council held at Rajgir under his patronage after Buddha's death.
Udayin 460–444 BC Founded Pataliputra in 455 BC at confluence of Ganga and Sone rivers. Shifted capital from Rajgir to Pataliputra. This is a KEY exam fact — Pataliputra founded by UDAYIN, not Ajatashatru.
Anuruddha, Munda, Nagadasaka Weak rulers Weak, parricide-prone rulers who led to a civil revolt. The dynasty ended when the people rose against the bloody dynastic feuding.
412 BC – 344 BC
3. Shishunaga Dynasty
TRANSITION
Shishunaga 412–394 BC Founded dynasty; was viceroy at Banaras. Capital at Vaishali (new) AND Rajgir — two capitals simultaneously. Greatest achievement: destroyed Pradyotas of Avanti (Ujjain) — ended 100-year rivalry between Magadha and Avanti.
Kalasoka (Kakavarna) 394–366 BC Organized 2nd Buddhist Council (383 BC) at Vaishali — to settle Vinaya Pitaka disputes. Shifted capital permanently to Pataliputra. Killed with a dagger by Mahapadma Nanda (per Harshacharita of Bana).
Ten sons of Kalasoka After 366 BC Last was Nandivardhan (Mahanandin). Killed by Mahapadmananda who founded the Nanda dynasty.
344 BC – 321 BC
4. Nanda Dynasty
LAST PRE-MAURYAN
Mahapadmananda 344–334 BC Founder. Killed last Shishunaga ruler. Title: Ekarat ("sole sovereign"). Also called Ugrasena (Mahabodhivamsa) and Mahapadmapati. Destroyed all other Kshatriya kingdoms. Per Puranas, his mother was a Shudra woman.
Dhana Nanda Last ruler ~321 BC Contemporary of Alexander the Great (327–326 BC). Greeks called him Agrammes or Xandremes. Despite enormous army (2 lakh infantry, 80,000 cavalry, 6,000 elephants, 8,000 chariots), lost to Chandragupta Maurya + Chanakya's strategy. Fabulously wealthy but unpopular.
⚠️

The 9 Nanda kings (per Mahabodhivamsa): Mahapadmananda, Panduka, Pandugati, Bhutapala, Rashtrapala, Govishanaka, Dashasiddhaka, Kaivarta, Dhana Nanda. BPSC sometimes asks "who was the last Nanda ruler?" Answer = Dhana Nanda.

⏳Master Timeline — All Four DynastiesCHRONOLOGICAL
6th C BC — BRIHADRATHA DYNASTY
Magadha Kingdom Established at Rajgir
Brihadratha (Rigveda) → Jarasandha (Mahabharata's villain, killed by Bhima) → Ripunjaya (last, killed by minister). Capital: Girivraja/Rajgir.
544 BC — HARYANKA: BIMBISARA ASCENDS
Age of Alliances + Conquest Begins
Bimbisara: 3 strategic marriages, permanent army, annexes Anga. Contemporary of both Buddha and Mahavira. Sends physician Jivaka to Ujjain. Known as Shrenika (Jain texts).
492 BC — AJATASHATRU KILLS BIMBISARA
Parricide Becomes Policy ⚠ Trap Q
Ajatashatru (Kunika) = Haryanka dynasty. Conquers Kosala and Vaishali. 1st Buddhist Council at Rajgir. Both Buddha (487 BC) and Mahavira (468 BC) attain Nirvana in his reign.
455 BC — PATALIPUTRA FOUNDED ⚠ Trap Q
Udayin Founds Modern Patna
Udayin (son of Ajatashatru) moves capital from Rajgir to Pataliputra at confluence of Ganga + Sone. This becomes India's most important city for 600+ years.
412 BC — SHISHUNAGA DYNASTY
100-Year Rivalry with Avanti Ends
Shishunaga destroys Avanti (Ujjain). 2nd Buddhist Council (383 BC) at Vaishali under Kalasoka. Capital permanently shifted to Pataliputra by Kalasoka.
344 BC — NANDA DYNASTY RISES
First "Low-Born" Dynasty — Most Powerful Army
Mahapadmananda (Ekarat) kills last Shishunaga. Accumulates legendary wealth. Controls Kalinga (brings Jina image). 9 kings in total.
327–326 BC
Alexander Invades India — Dhana Nanda's Fear
Alexander reaches Punjab. His generals hear of Dhana Nanda's army — refuse to cross Beas river. Alexander turns back. Dhana Nanda (Agrammes) is contemporary of Alexander.
321 BC — END OF PRE-MAURYAN ERA
Chandragupta + Chanakya Overthrow Dhana Nanda
Chandragupta Maurya with Chanakya's strategy defeats Dhana Nanda. Maurya dynasty begins. Pre-Mauryan era ends. Empire phase of Bihar history starts.

Result

Magadha ready for India's First Empire — Maurya Dynasty (321 BC)

200 years of four dynasties created the infrastructure: capital city (Pataliputra), standing army, river trade routes, iron weapons, and diplomatic networks — all of which Chandragupta Maurya inherited and used to build India's first pan-Indian empire.

📝PYQ PracticeSOLVED
BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2011
What was the name of the dynasty of Ajatashatru?
(a) Maurya
(b) Haryanka
(c) Nanda
(d) Gupta
✓ CORRECT: (b) Haryanka

Ajatashatru belonged to the Haryanka dynasty — founded by his father Bimbisara in 544 BC. This is one of the most frequently wrong-answered BPSC questions. Students confuse Ajatashatru with Maurya (Chandragupta's dynasty) because he was an early powerful Magadha king. Remember: Ajatashatru's childhood name was Kunika; he killed Bimbisara to ascend the throne.

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2008
Which ruler was the first to establish his capital at Patliputra?
(a) Ajatashatru
(b) Kalasoka
(c) Udayin
(d) Kanishka
✓ CORRECT: (c) Udayin

⚠️ Major trap — many students answer Ajatashatru or Chandragupta Maurya. The correct answer is Udayin (son of Ajatashatru, 460–444 BC) who founded Pataliputra in 455 BC at the confluence of the Ganga and Sone rivers. Kalasoka later shifted the capital from Rajgir/Vaishali permanently to Pataliputra — but it was Udayin who first established it.

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2001
Which ruler of Magadha was the contemporary of Alexander the Great?
(a) Mahapadmananda
(b) Dhana Nanda
(c) Chandragupta Maurya
(d) Kushan
✓ CORRECT: (b) Dhana Nanda

When Alexander invaded India (327–326 BC), Dhana Nanda was the ruler of Magadha. Greeks referred to him as "Agrammes" or "Xandremes." Alexander's generals, after hearing about Dhana Nanda's enormous army (2 lakh infantry, 80,000 cavalry, 6,000 war elephants), refused to march further east. Alexander turned back without ever facing Dhana Nanda. Note: Chandragupta Maurya OVERTHREW Dhana Nanda — so Chandragupta's reign started AFTER Alexander left.

✅Concept CheckTEST YOURSELF
1. Shishunaga dynasty's greatest achievement was defeating which rival kingdom — ending a 100-year rivalry?
✓ Correct! Shishunaga's greatest achievement was the destruction of the Pradyotas of Avanti (Ujjain) — ending the 100-year long rivalry between Magadha and Avanti. Avanti had been Magadha's most serious competitor. After this, Avanti became part of the Magadha empire and remained so till the end of Mauryan rule.
✗ Answer is (B) Avanti (Ujjain). Shishunaga broke the 100-year Magadha-Avanti rivalry by destroying the Pradyota dynasty of Avanti. Note: Ajatashatru (Haryanka dynasty) had conquered Vajji; Bimbisara conquered Kosala through marriage.
2. Who was the founder of the Nanda dynasty who assumed the title "Ekarat"?
✓ Correct! Mahapadmananda founded the Nanda dynasty by killing last Shishunaga ruler Mahanandin. He assumed the title "Ekarat" (sole sovereign) — meaning he claimed to be the only independent ruler who had destroyed all other Kshatriya kingdoms. He was also called Ugrasena (Mahabodhivamsa). Dhana Nanda was the LAST, not first, Nanda king.
✗ Answer is (A) Mahapadmananda. He was the FOUNDER (first) of the Nanda dynasty. Dhana Nanda was the LAST Nanda king — don't confuse them. Ekarat = "sole sovereign" = Mahapadmananda's title.
MAINS GS-I150 words
Trace the rise of Magadha as the dominant power in ancient India through the four pre-Mauryan dynasties. What were the key factors that enabled this rise?
Introduction

Magadha's dominance over ancient India did not emerge suddenly with the Mauryas — it was the product of 200 years of strategic accumulation across four dynasties.

Dynasty-wise Progression
  • Brihadratha: Established Magadha as a kingdom at Rajgir. Jarasandha extended it militarily.
  • Haryanka: Bimbisara's marriage alliances brought Kashi (dowry), Licchavi alliance, and Punjab connection without wars. Ajatashatru conquered Vaishali using espionage.
  • Shishunaga: Eliminated 100-year rival Avanti; Udayin had already moved capital to strategic Pataliputra (455 BC).
  • Nanda: Accumulated massive wealth and the largest army in Asia — setting the stage even if they lost to Chandragupta.
Key Enabling Factors
  • Iron mines near Rajgir → superior weapons
  • Fertile Gangetic plains → agricultural surplus → revenue
  • River networks (Ganga, Sone, Gandak) → trade and military mobility
  • Strategic capital: Pataliputra at river confluence
  • Policy innovations: permanent army (Bimbisara), espionage (Ajatashatru)
Conclusion

When Chandragupta Maurya inherited Magadha in 321 BC, he did not build from scratch — he stood on foundations laid by four dynasties over two centuries. Bihar's geography and these political experiments made India's first empire possible.
