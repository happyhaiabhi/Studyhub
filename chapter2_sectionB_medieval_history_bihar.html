📜 Sources of Medieval History of Bihar (2.7)

Minhas-us-Siraj (Tabakat-i-Nasiri) stayed IN Bihar — firsthand account of Bakhtiyar Khalji's attacks. Abul Fazl wrote Akbarnama (NOT Ain-i-Akbari alone). Bhabua inscription = Sher Shah. Bedibun inscription = Muhammad bin Tughlaq.

Sufi Literature Sources

  • Tajkira, Malkujat, Maktubaat — Sufi writings providing historical account of medieval Bihar
  • Coin minting definite proof comes from Tirhut-urf-Tughlaqpur (not Munger or Gaya)
  • Arabic and Persian inscriptions found predominantly on walls of mosques, tombs and imambaras — scattered all over Bihar Sharif, Sasaram, Patna

⚔️ Early Medieval Bihar — Palas, Senas, Karnatas (2.8)

Gopala founded Pala dynasty AND Odantapura Vihara. Dharmapala founded Vikramshila (NOT Gopala). Harisimhadeva was last Karnata king — defeated by Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq (NOT Iltutmish).

Pala Dynasty

Founder
Gopala — ended political anarchy. Founded Odantapura Vihara (modern Bihar Sharif) 🔥
Greatest Pala
Dharmapala — founded Vikramshila Mahavihara (Antichak, Bhagalpur). Built Sonapura Vihara in N. Bengal. 🔥
Devapala
Conquered Utkala (Orissa) and Pragjyotisha (Assam). Established relations with Balaputradeva of Suvarnadvipa. 🔥
Devapala's Inscriptions
Found at Ghosrawan, Hilsa, Nalanda and Munger
Munger Copper Plate
Belongs to Devapala — states Dharmapala left kingdom free from troubles
Narayanapala's successor
Rajyapala — ruled at least 32 years, kept Magadha under control

Pala Dynasty — Key Facts 🔥

  • Dharmapala = first Pala king to assume full imperial titles: Maharajadhiraja Parmeshvara
  • Pratiharas revived under Nagabhatta II who defeated Dharmapala near Mudgagiri (Munger)
  • Badal Pillar Inscription mentions Surapala as successor of Devapala vs Bhagalpur Inscription which says Vigrahapala I — contested succession
  • Devapala established friendly relations with Balaputradeva, king of Suvarnadvipa (Sumatra)
Pala order: Gopala (founder) → Dharmapala (greatest, Vikramshila) → Devapala (conqueror) → Narayanapala → Rajyapala. "Good Dads Don't Neglect Responsibilities!"

Sena Dynasty

Sena Kings — Quick Reference

Founder

Vijayasena
Son of Hemantsena. Long reign of 60+ years. Brought family into limelight.
Ballalsena

Lakshmansena

Karnata Dynasty of Mithila

Karnata Dynasty 🔥

  • Founded by Nanyadeva in AD 1097 — capital at Simraon, Champaran
  • Called Karnataka Keshava. Ruled ~50 years (1097–1147). Expert in music.
  • Successors: Gangadeva → Narsimhadeva (1188–1227) → Harisimhadeva
  • Harisimhadeva = last great Karnata king. Defeated by Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq (1324-25). Fled to Nepal.
  • After Harisimha fled, daughter married into Nepal royal family — Karnata blood continued in Nepal.
Karnata: Founded at Simraon (Champaran) by Nanyadeva 1097 AD. Ended when Harisimhadeva fled to Nepal after Ghiyasuddin's defeat. "Nanyadeva Started, Harisimha Fled Nepal!"

🏰 Bihar Under the Delhi Sultanate (2.9)

Bakhtiyar Khalji was killed by his OWN lieutenant Ali Mardan Khalji — NOT defeated in battle. First Delhi Sultan to invade Bihar = ILTUTMISH (1225 AD). Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq ended Karnata dynasty — NOT Iltutmish!

⚔️ Rise of Afghans in Bihar (2.10)

Sher Shah's childhood name = FARID. Father Hasan Khan was jagirdar of SASARAM (not Patna). Sher Shah defeated Humayun at CHAUSA (1539) THEN KANAUJ (1540) — in that order. Died at KALINJAR (1545) from accidental gunpowder explosion.
Darya Khan Nuhani
Head of Bihar affairs 1495–1522. Loyal to Sikander Lodhi but independent stature.
Nuhani founder
Bahar Khan Nuhani — rose after Ibrahim Lodhi was killed at Panipat (1526)
Sher Shah's real name
Farid. Title 'Sher Khan' given by Muhammad Nuhani after killing a tiger. 🔥
Father's jagir
Hasan Khan — jagirdar of Sasaram. "Sasaram = administrative laboratory" 🔥
Fort of Chunar
Obtained by Sher Shah by marrying Lado Malka, widow of Taj Khan Sarangkhani
Last Afghan ruler of Bihar
Dawood Khan Kararani — defeated by Akbar at Battle of Rajmahal 🔥

Sher Shah's Battles with Humayun 🔥

Battle of Chausa (1539)
First defeat of Humayun. Humayun barely escaped. Sher Shah showed strategic patience — drew Mughals into Bengal first.
Battle of Kanauj/Bilgram (1540)
Decisive defeat of Humayun. Humayun fled India. Sher Shah occupied Bihar and Bengal, marched to Agra.
Death at Kalinjar (1545)
Untimely death — accidental gunpowder explosion while besieging Kalinjar fort. Succeeded by son Islam Shah.
Chausa (1539) → Kanauj (1540) → Kalinjar death (1545). "Chausa Kicked, Kanauj Knocked out, Kalinjar Killed him!"

Sher Shah's Administrative Reforms 🔥

  • Introduced Sarkar (district) unit for the first time in Indian history
  • Land revenue = Zabit system — based on measurement. Made by Todarmal.
  • Introduced silver coin Rupai (Rupee) of 178 grains — uniform and standard 🔥
  • Built Grand Trunk Road (Sadak-e-Azam) — Bengal to Indus
  • Built a fort in Patna and made Patna the capital of Bihar — referred to in Tarikh-i-Dawoodi by Abdullah
  • Considered predecessor of Akbar in public works and administration
  • Tomb at Sasaram — octagonal mausoleum 🔥

Chero & Ujjaini Dynasties

Chero Dynasty
Tribal state after Pala decline. Kukumchand Jharap (1587-1607) = head of Cheros of Bhojpur, chased Ujjainnis.
Ujjaini Dynasty
Originally Rajputs from Ujjain (Dhar/Malwa). Fled after Alauddin Khalji's conquest. Given jagir of South Gangetic Plain by Chero kings.
Kararani Dynasty
Taj Khan Kararani founded Afghan state after Sher Shah. Sulaiman Kararani = major ruler. Dawood Khan (son) = last Afghan ruler.
Construction — NOT Sher Shah
Ibadat Khana was NOT built by Sher Shah (it was Akbar). Know what he did NOT do.

🕌 Bihar During Mughal Rule (2.11)

Akbar merged Bihar+Bengal into Mughal empire in 1576 (Battle of Rajmahal). Raja Man Singh = appointed by AKBAR (1587). First Mughal Prince as governor = Prince PARVEZ (by Jahangir, 1621). Guru Gobind Singh born at PATNA in 1666!

Akbar and Bihar (1556–1605)

  • Daud Khan (last Afghan) defeated at Battle of Rajmahal by Akbar. Bihar+Bengal merged into Mughal empire in AD 1576. 🔥
  • First governor: Munim Khan
  • Khan-i-Azam → transferred to Malwa 1583 → succeeded by Sayeed Khan Chagta
  • Raja Man Singh appointed governor of Bihar in AD 1587. Built Kathotia gate at Rohtasgarh. 🔥
  • Last governor under Akbar = Asif Khan

Jahangir and Bihar (1605–1627) 🔥

  • Succession of governors: Beg Bahadur → Islam Khan → Afzal Khan → Jafar Khan → Ibrahim Khan → Jahangir Quli → Muqarrab Khan → Parvez
  • Nur Jahan passed through Darbhanga during governorship of Beg Bahadur
  • First Mughal Prince as governor = Prince Parvez (son of Jahangir) — appointed AD 1621 🔥
  • Ibrahim Khan = attacked Kukradesh and arrested Durjan Sal
  • Nazar Bahadur constructed Sangi Masjid (Pathar-ki-Masjid) in Patna
Nur Jahan passed through Darbhanga when? During BEG BAHADUR's governorship. "Beg Bahadur Brought Nur Jahan through Bihar!"

Shah Jahan and Aurangzeb

Shah Jahan (1627-58)
First governor: Khan-i-Alam. Shaista Khan = notable governor. Saif Khan built Shahi Idgah in Patna City.
Aurangzeb (1658-1707)
First governor: Daud Khan Quraishi. Defeated Cheros of Palamau. European travellers Tavernier and Bernier visited Bihar during Jan Nisar Khan's tenure.
Murshid Quli Khan
Dewan of Bihar under Prince Azim (grandson of Aurangzeb). Strict and hardworking. Relations with lazy prince became restrained.
First Mughal oath in Patna
Farrukhsiyar — first Mughal ruler to be sworn in at Patna 🔥

Sikhs in Bihar 🔥

  • Guru Nanak Dev visited Gaya, Rajgir, Patna, Munger, Bhagalpur, Kahalgoan — propagated Sikhism
  • Guru Gobind Singh — 10th and LAST Sikh Guru. Born at Patna in 1666. 🔥
  • Guru Teg Bahadur on way to Assam stayed at Patna — son Gobind born here
  • Guru Teg Bahadur executed in 1675 in Delhi
  • Guru Gobind Singh announced establishment of Khalsa on 1st Baishak, 1699
  • Birthplace = Takht Sri Patna Sahib Gurudwara (Patna Sahib) 🔥
Guru Gobind Singh: Born PATNA 1666, Khalsa 1699, last Sikh Guru. "Patna 1666 = Tenth Guru's birth, 1699 = Khalsa birth!"
