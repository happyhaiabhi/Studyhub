MODULE 04·Ancient Bihar

Buddhism & Jainism
Born in Bihar

The 6th century BC Bihar produced the two most world-shaping religious revolutions in Indian history — simultaneously. The same soil, the same rivers, the same cities — but two very different paths to the same question: how does one end suffering?

BPSC Special Mains GS-I 6th Century BC ⚠ Trap: 4 Buddhist Councils — locations, patrons, presidencies
🎭Story HookSTART HERE

The year is 528 BC. A 35-year-old man sits beneath a Peepal tree on the banks of the Niranjana river in what is now Bodh Gaya, Bihar. He has been meditating for weeks. He has renounced a kingdom, a wife, a child, and every comfort known to royalty. And then — in the stillness of that Bihar night — something shifts.

He becomes the Buddha. The Awakened One. The man who will teach a philosophy so revolutionary it will spread across Asia and still has 500 million followers today — all because of one tree, one river, one night in Bihar.

Twelve years earlier and 200 km north in Vaishali, another aristocrat had made the same renunciation. Vardhamana Mahavira, aged 30, left his palace in Kundagram (Bihar). He would spend the next 12 years in extreme meditation and austerity, attaining Kaivalya at age 42. He became the Jina — the Conqueror. His teachings would become Jainism's final and most complete form.

💡 Dramatic Fact: Both Buddha and Mahavira were Kshatriyas from Bihar, both renounced royal life within decades of each other, both preached non-violence — yet created two distinct religions that survive 2,500 years later.
🧠Core Concept — Buddhism & Jainism Side by SidePARALLEL FOUNDERS
☸ Buddhism
THE MIDDLE PATH
Born: Siddhartha Gautama, 563 BC, Lumbini (Nepal)
Clan: Sakya Kshatriya; Father: Suddhodana; Mother: Mahamaya; Wife: Yashodhara
Renunciation (age 29): Called Mahabhinishkramana
Enlightenment (age 35): 528 BC, Bodh Gaya, under Peepal (Bodhi) tree on Niranjana river bank
First sermon: Sarnath (Dharmachakra Pravartana)
Last sermon: Vaishali, Bihar
Mahaparinirvana: Kushinagar (capital of Mallas), 487 BC
Core teaching: 4 Noble Truths, 8-fold Path; Middle Path between extreme luxury and extreme asceticism
⚛ Jainism
THE PATH OF CONQUERORS
Born: Vardhamana Mahavira, 540 BC, Kundagrama near Vaishali, Bihar
Clan: Jnatrika Kshatriya; Father: Siddhartha; Mother: Trishala (Licchavi princess, sister of King Chetaka)
Renunciation (age 30): Left palace; called Nirgranthas
Kaivalya (age 42): At banks of Rijupalika river, under a Sal tree
First sermon: Vipulgiri, Rajgir; First disciple: Jamali
Nirvana: 468 BC, Pavapuri near Rajgir, Bihar
24th Tirthankar: Rishabhdeva was 1st; Parshvanatha was 23rd; Mahavira was last (24th)
Three Gems (Triratna): Right Faith + Right Knowledge + Right Conduct = Moksha
⏳Timeline — Key Events in BiharCHRONOLOGICAL
599/540 BC
Mahavira Born at Kundagrama, Vaishali
Born to Siddhartha and Trishala (Licchavi princess). Part of the Jnatrika clan — same region where the Licchavi republic flourished. Bihar produced the 24th and final Tirthankar of Jainism.
563 BC
Buddha Born at Lumbini (Nepal) Near Bihar border
Born Siddhartha Gautama in Sakya Kshatriya clan. His entire spiritual journey — enlightenment, preaching, teaching — took place primarily in Bihar. Lumbini is in Nepal near Bihar's northern border.
528 BC
Buddha Attains Enlightenment — Bodh Gaya, Bihar
Meditating under Peepal (Bodhi) tree on banks of Niranjana river at Uruvela (now Bodh Gaya). Became the Tathagata ("Thus Come One"). First sermon: Dharmachakra Pravartana at Sarnath.
510 BC (approx)
Mahavira Attains Kaivalya (age 42)
At banks of Rijupalika river under a Sal tree. Conquered misery and happiness → became Jina (Conqueror) → Mahavira. Gave first sermon at Vipulgiri (Rajgir), Bihar. First disciple was Jamali.
487 BC
Buddha's Mahaparinirvana — Kushinagar ⚠ NOT Bihar
Buddha's death (Mahaparinirvana) occurred at Kushinagar — capital of the Mallas (now in UP, not Bihar). Last sermon was given in Vaishali, Bihar. Sariputta (one of Buddha's greatest disciples) was born near Rajgir, Bihar.
483 BC
1st Buddhist Council — Rajgir, Bihar
Held at Saptaparni Caves, Rajgir. Patron: Ajatashatru. President: Mahakasyapa. Compiled Buddha's teachings (Sutta) and rules (Vinaya).
468 BC
Mahavira Attains Nirvana — Pavapuri, Bihar
Mahavira's Nirvana at Pavapuri near Rajgir — this is Bihar. Pavapuri is where Jains from across the world come to pay homage. Still one of the most important Jain pilgrimage sites.
383 BC
2nd Buddhist Council — Vaishali, Bihar
100 years after Buddha. Patron: Kalasoka. President: Sabakami. Dispute over Vinaya Pitaka (10 points of discipline). Held at Vaishali.
3rd C BC
1st Jain Council — Patliputra
Led by Sthulabhadra. Jainism splits into Svetambaras and Digambaras at this council. Held at Patliputra (Bihar).
250 BC
3rd Buddhist Council — Patliputra, Bihar
Patron: Ashoka. President: Moggaliputta Tissa. Abhidhamma Pitaka established. Buddhism spread to Sri Lanka, Southeast Asia, West Asia. Major milestone in Buddhist history.
78 AD
4th Buddhist Council — Kashmir ⚠ NOT Bihar
Held at Kundalavana, Kashmir. Patron: Kanishka (Kushana). Asvaghosha participated. Mahayana Buddhism emerged at this council. This is the only Buddhist council NOT held in Bihar.
5th Century AD
2nd Jain Council — Vallabhi
Led by Devardhi Gani Kshmasramana. Final compilation of 12 Angas and 12 Upangas. Held at Vallabhi (Gujarat — NOT Bihar). Important to note this council is outside Bihar.

📚Tripitaka — Three Baskets of Buddhist TextsCORE TEXTS
Vinayapitaka
BASKET OF DISCIPLINE
Rules and regulations of monastic life for Buddhist monks. Compiled at 1st Council. Subject of dispute at 2nd Council.
Suttapitaka
BASKET OF DISCOURSES
Collection of Buddha's sermons and dialogues. Contains Anguttara Nikaya, Digha Nikaya — which mention the 16 Mahajanapadas.
Abhidhammapitaka
BASKET OF PHILOSOPHY
Philosophical and psychological interpretation of Buddhist doctrine. Compiled/established at the 3rd Buddhist Council (Patliputra, Ashoka's time).
⚛24 Tirthankaras — The Three Key OnesJAIN EXAM FOCUS
1st TIRTHANKAR
Rishabhdeva
Also called Adinatha. First Tirthankar. Considered founder of Jain path. Also mentioned in Hindu texts.
23rd TIRTHANKAR
Parshvanatha
Lived ~250 years before Mahavira. Taught 4 vows (ahimsa, satya, asteya, aparigraha). Historical figure accepted by scholars.
24th TIRTHANKAR (LAST)
Mahavira
Born Kundagrama (Vaishali, Bihar). Added 5th vow — Brahmacharya. Completed and systematised Jainism. Nirvana at Pavapuri, Bihar.

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2018
After the Mahaparinirvana of Lord Buddha, the first Buddhist Council was held at —
(a) Rajgriha
(b) Gaya
(c) Patliputra
(d) Vaishali
✓ CORRECT: (a) Rajgriha (Rajgir)

1st Buddhist Council (483 BC) was held at Rajgriha (modern Rajgir, Bihar) in the Saptaparni Caves. Patron: Ajatashatru; President: Mahakasyapa. Patliputra = 3rd Council; Vaishali = 2nd Council. This is frequently swapped — remember R-V-P-K!

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2011
Where was Mahaveer Swami born?
(a) Kundagram (Vaishali)
(b) Patliputra
(c) Magadha
(d) Rajgir
✓ CORRECT: (a) Kundagram (Vaishali)

Mahavira was born in 540 BC at Kundagrama near Vaishali, Bihar. His mother Trishala was a Licchavi princess (sister of King Chetaka of Vaishali). He attained Kaivalya near the Rijupalika river and attained Nirvana at Pavapuri near Rajgir. So birth = Vaishali; Nirvana = Pavapuri (Bihar).

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2018
The salvation place of Mahavirji (Nirvana of Mahavira) is located at —
(a) Maner
(b) Rajgir
(c) Pavapuri
(d) Jalan Fort
✓ CORRECT: (c) Pavapuri

Mahavira attained Nirvana (Moksha) in 468 BC at Pavapuri near Rajgriha (Rajgir). Pavapuri is in Nalanda district, Bihar. The Jal Mandir temple is built here — a white marble temple in the middle of a lake, one of Jainism's most sacred pilgrimage sites. Don't confuse: Rajgir = where he gave first sermon (not Nirvana).

MAINS GS-I150 words
Bihar is called the birthplace of Buddhism and Jainism. Discuss the contribution of Bihar to the rise and spread of these two religions, citing specific sites, events, and personalities.
Introduction

The 6th century BC Bihar witnessed the simultaneous emergence of Buddhism and Jainism — two philosophical revolutions that have shaped Asian civilization for 2,500 years.

Buddhism — Bihar's Role
  • Bodh Gaya: Siddhartha attained enlightenment (528 BC) under the Bodhi tree — the sacred centre of Buddhism globally
  • Rajgir: Buddha taught here; 1st Buddhist Council (483 BC) held at Saptaparni Caves
  • Vaishali: Buddha delivered his last sermon; 2nd Buddhist Council (383 BC)
  • Nalanda: Birthplace of Sariputta, one of Buddha's greatest disciples
  • Patliputra: 3rd Council under Ashoka (250 BC) led to global spread of Buddhism
Jainism — Bihar's Role
  • Kundagrama (Vaishali): Birthplace of Mahavira (540 BC), the 24th and final Tirthankar
  • Rajgir (Vipulgiri): Mahavira's first sermon; first disciple Jamali
  • Pavapuri: Mahavira's Nirvana (468 BC) — one of Jainism's holiest sites
  • Patliputra: 1st Jain Council (3rd C BC) where Jainism split into Svetambara and Digambara sects
Conclusion

Bihar's soil was uniquely fertile for religious revolution — questioning Brahmanical orthodoxy, embracing non-violence, and developing democratic philosophies. The state's rivers, forests, and cities provided the perfect backdrop for two of humanity's most enduring spiritual traditions.
