Plassey vs Buxar — Comparison 🔥 BPSC Frequent

Battle of Plassey (1757)
Defeat of Nawab of Bengal (Siraj-ud-Daulah) only. Mir Jafar made new governor.
Battle of Buxar (1764)
Defeat of Nawab of Bengal + Nawab of Awadh + submission of Mughal Emperor. Far more significant.
BUXAR > PLASSEY: "Plassey = 1 Nawab, Buxar = 1 Nawab + 1 Emperor + 1 Nawab of Awadh. Bigger enemy, bigger victory!"

Treaty of Allahabad & Diwani Rights

  • Shah Alam II got: districts of Kora & Allahabad + annual tribute of ₹26 lakhs
  • In return: Firman dated August 12 & 19, 1765 → East India Company got Diwani rights of Bengal, Bihar and Orissa
  • British became virtual masters of Bengal, Bihar and Orissa

Clive's Congress at Chapra

Robert Clive convened a Congress at Chapra attended by Shuja-ud-daulah, envoys of the Emperor, the Jat Raja and Rohilla chiefs. They entered into a mutual defence treaty against Maratha aggression. Colonel Smith was posted at Sasaram to watch Maratha movements.

💡 Wahabi Movement — Key Facts

  • Founder: Syed Ahmad (called it a religious + political movement)
  • Bihar centre: Sadikpore family of Patna — Wilayat Ali, Inayat Ali, Shah Muhammad Hussain, Abdullah
  • Patna Trials of 1864: M. Ahmadullah arrested Nov 5, 1864 — sentenced to death, commuted to transportation for life → sent to Port Blair (Andamans)
  • Duration: 40 years active in Patna (1828–1868)
Wahabi leaders: "Wilayat = Wisdom, Inayat = Action — elder was wise, younger was brave"

👥 Key Persons — Freedom Movement

Khudiram Bose — Bihar's Youngest Revolutionary

Khudiram Bose threw a bomb at the carriage of Muzaffarpur Judge Kingsford in April 1908. He was hanged on August 11, 1908 — the youngest revolutionary hanged by the British.

Khudiram Bose: "Youngest revolutionary — hanged at just 18! August 11, 1908"

Patna Secretariat Martyrs — August 11, 1942

During the Quit India Movement, 7 students hoisted the national flag on Patna Secretariat: Umakant Prasad Singh, Ram Anand Singh, Satish Prasad Jha, Jagannath Singh, Debi Sinha, Rajendra Singh, Ramgovind Singh. All 7 were killed by police fire.

"7 students, 7 heroes — August 11, 1942 — never forget"

JP's Hazaribagh Escape — Diwali 1942

Jayaprakash Narayan (JP) escaped from Hazaribagh Central Jail on the night of Diwali 1942 along with five other political prisoners — one of the most dramatic jailbreaks in Bihar's freedom story.

"Diwali night escape — the most dramatic jailbreak in Bihar's freedom story!"
