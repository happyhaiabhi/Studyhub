🏭 European Companies in Bihar 🔥 BPSC Frequent

English came FIRST (1620) BUT closed in 1621. Dutch (1632) had the first CONTINUOUS factory. Don't confuse "first to arrive" vs "first continuous presence"!
🇵🇹 Portuguese — First
First Europeans in Bihar. Factory at Hooghly (Bengal). Frequently visited Patna via water channels & roads for saltpetre, cotton, indigo.
🇳🇱 Dutch — 1632
Factory in Patna in 1632. Site = present northern building of Patna College.
🇬🇧 English EIC — 1620/1651
Factory at Alamganj, Patna in 1620 for saltpetre. Closed 1621. Revived 1651 at Gulzar Bagh = now Govt. Printing Press.
🇩🇰 Danes — 1774–75
Factory at Nepali Kothi, Patna in 1774–75. Last Europeans to arrive in Bihar.

Bihar's Trade Products

  • Saltpetre (potassium nitrate) — used in making ammunition. Bihar was #1 source. Hajipur, Tirhut, Saran, Purnea = major centres.
  • Cotton textiles and Indigo — also important for European trade.
  • Noniyas were the saltpetre workers — exploitation led to Noniya Revolt (1770–1800).
European order: P-D-E-D = Portuguese → Dutch (1632) → English (1620 first, 1651 revival) → Danes (1774). "Please Don't Eat Dinner" — in order of arrival!

👑 Alivardi Khan and Bihar

How Bihar Came Under Bengal Completely

  • Prince Mirza Ahmad appointed Shuja-ud-din (Bengal governor) as Bihar deputy — but neither came to Bihar!
  • Shuja-ud-din appointed Alivardi Khan on his behalf → Bihar fully under Bengal.
  • Alivardi Khan won loyalty of Hindus and Rohilla Chiefs.
  • Subdued: Ujjainis, Rajas of Bettiah and Darbhanga, Sunder Singh (Raja of Tikari).

Why Mir Qasim Fought — Background

  • Mir Jafar removed from masnad in 1760; Mir Qasim (son-in-law) appointed.
  • Mir Qasim captured Raja Ram Narayan → transferred to Munger fort → ordered drowned in Ganges.
  • Shah Alam II invaded Bihar (1761, 3rd time) → defeated → escorted by Carnac from Gaya to Patna.
  • Conflict cause: Mir Qasim wanted to stop misuse of dastaks (free passes) by English traders giving unfair trade advantage.
Battle of Buxar: 22/10/1764 — "22 October like Dussehra season — a grand final battle showdown!"

Plassey vs Buxar Comparison 🔥 BPSC Frequent

Battle of Plassey (1757)
Only Nawab of Bengal (Siraj-ud-Daulah) defeated. Mir Jafar made new governor. Smaller in scope.
Battle of Buxar (1764)
Nawab of Bengal + Nawab of Awadh + Mughal Emperor Shah Alam II all submitted. Far greater historical significance.
BUXAR > PLASSEY: "Plassey = 1 Nawab; Buxar = 1 Nawab + 1 Emperor + 1 Nawab of Awadh. Bigger enemies, bigger victory, bigger significance!"

Treaty of Allahabad & Diwani Rights 🔥

  • Shah Alam II received: districts of Kora & Allahabad + annual tribute of ₹26 lakhs.
  • In return: Firman dated August 12 & 19, 1765 → EIC got Diwani rights of Bengal, Bihar & Orissa.
  • EIC became virtual masters of Bengal, Bihar and Orissa.
  • First English magistrate of Patna: Charles Francis Grand.
  • Golghar in Patna constructed during: Lord Cornwallis.

💡 Wahabi Movement — Full Details 🔥

  • "Wahabi" = disciple of Abdul Wahab of Najd (Arabia) — term used by British for propaganda.
  • Four Khalifas appointed by Syed Ahmad to manage the Bihar movement.
  • Wilayat Ali (elder): political wisdom & experience. Inayat Ali (younger): brave soldier & forceful preacher. Two brothers, two roles.
  • Sittana (NWFP) = military headquarters of Wahabi colony; Patna = base camp supplying money & recruits.
  • 1850–58: Wahabis kept frontier tribes in active hostility. British sent expeditions against them.
  • Patna Trials 1864: M. Ahmadullah arrested Nov 5, 1864 → sentenced to death → commuted → transported to Port Blair (Andamans).
Wahabi: "Wilayat = Wisdom (elder, political mind), Inayat = Action (younger, brave fighter)." The Patna centre ran for 40 years — 1828 to 1868!

🌿 Champaran Satyagraha Deep Dive 🔥

  • Two indigo systems: Zerait and Assamiwar
  • Tinkathia system: Tenant must cultivate indigo on 3 kathas per bigha (= 3/20th of land) — most prevalent method in Champaran
  • Raj Kumar Shukla met Gandhi at Lucknow Congress, December 1916
  • Gandhi reached Muzaffarpur: April 10, 1917 → Motihari: April 15, 1917
  • Govt withdrew case vs Gandhi: April 20. This was the first phase of success.
  • Champaran Enquiry Committee → Champaran Agrarian Act passed May 1, 1918
  • Anushilan Samiti branch in Bihar: Patna
Tinkathia: "TIN = 3, KATHA = unit of land. 3/20th of your land forcibly growing indigo. TIN kathas = their burden!"

🏛️ Creation of Modern Bihar

  • Separate Bihar movement credited to: Dr. Sachchidanand Sinha and Mahesh Narain
  • George V declared separation at Delhi Durbar: December 12, 1911
  • Bihar & Orissa province came into existence: April 1, 1912
  • Bihar & Orissa separated: 1936 (under GOI Act 1935)
  • Jharkhand from Bihar: November 15, 2000
1912 = DOUBLE significance year: "Bihar born as separate province + INC Bankipur session = Bihar's coming of age! One-Nine-One-Two = Bihar's birthday!"
