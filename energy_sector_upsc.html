Policy & Schemes — Regulatory Framework & Government Initiatives

Exploration Licensing Policy Timeline

1999
NELP (New Exploration Licensing Policy) — Replaced APM, brought PSCs (Production Sharing Contracts). Allowed 100% FDI in E&P. Multiple rounds (NELP I–IX) till 2012.
2016
HELP (Hydrocarbon Exploration and Licensing Policy) — Replaced NELP. Single licence for all hydrocarbons (oil, gas, CBM, shale). Revenue Sharing Contract instead of PSC. Open Acreage Licensing Programme (OALP).
2016
DSF (Discovered Small Field) Policy — To monetize 69+ small/marginal fields left undeveloped by ONGC/OIL. Revenue sharing basis.
2020
OALP (Open Acreage Licensing Programme) — Companies can select their own exploration blocks from national data repository. More flexible than previous rounds.

Fuel Subsidy Reforms

📉 Subsidy Reform Timeline — UPSC Critical
2010
Petrol prices DECONTROLLED — linked to market. Diesel still subsidized.
2014
Diesel prices DECONTROLLED under Modi govt (falling crude prices helped). Major fiscal reform.
2015
LPG DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer) — PAHAL scheme. Subsidy directly to Aadhaar-linked bank accounts. 200+ million beneficiaries. Reduced leakage dramatically.
2022
Kerosene subsidy largely eliminated — PDS kerosene phased out as LPG reaches rural areas via PMUY.
🎯 MAINS ANGLE — Fuel Subsidy Reform
  • Subsidy reform freed fiscal space — reduced subsidy burden from 1.5% of GDP to 0.3%
  • DBT for LPG is world's largest DBT programme — cited as model by World Bank
  • Challenge: Price decontrol hurts poor when crude prices spike (2022 example)
  • Political economy: Fuel price hikes near elections become sensitive

The 5 Critical Oil Chokepoints

1. 🔴 Strait of Hormuz — MOST CRITICAL
  • Between Iran and Oman/UAE
  • 21 million barrels/day = 20% of global oil
  • All Gulf oil (Saudi, UAE, Iraq, Kuwait, Iran) passes here
  • Narrows to just 33 km at its narrowest
  • Iran repeatedly threatens to close it during tensions
India's Exposure
~65% of India's crude comes through Hormuz. Any disruption = crisis for India.
2. 🟠 Strait of Malacca
  • Between Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia
  • ~16 million barrels/day + massive LNG flows
  • Gateway for Middle East oil → China, Japan, South Korea
  • Narrows to 2.8 km at Philip Channel
  • Piracy concern; China's "Malacca Dilemma"
China's Dilemma
China relies 80%+ on Malacca for energy imports → vulnerability. India's Andaman Islands flank this route → strategic advantage.
3. 🔵 Suez Canal
  • Connects Red Sea to Mediterranean
  • ~5.5 million barrels/day + 8% global trade
  • Cuts distance Middle East → Europe by 7,000 km vs Cape route
  • 2021: Ever Given blockage — disrupted global supply chains
  • Houthi attacks 2023-24 forced ships to reroute via Cape
4. 🟡 Bab el-Mandeb Strait
  • Between Yemen and Djibouti/Eritrea (Red Sea exit)
  • ~6 million barrels/day
  • Houthi attacks 2023-24 made this zone dangerous
  • Ships now going Cape route → higher costs for India
  • India's Operation Sankalp (2019): Naval patrol here
5. ⚫ Turkish Straits (Bosphorus + Dardanelles)
  • Connects Black Sea → Mediterranean
  • ~3 million barrels/day (mostly Russian + Caspian oil)
  • Istanbul is on Bosphorus — highest density strait
  • Turkey controls access — geopolitical leverage

PYQs & Master Mnemonics

UPSC Prelims PYQs — Energy Sector

UPSC PRELIMS 2019
With reference to India's oil sector, consider the following statements:
1. India is a net importer of petroleum products
2. The refining capacity of India exceeds its domestic demand for petroleum products
Which of the above is/are correct?
(a) 1 only   (b) 2 only   (c) Both 1 and 2   (d) Neither
Answer: (b) 2 only
Statement 1 is WRONG: India is a NET EXPORTER of petroleum products (refined goods). It imports crude oil, but refines more than it consumes → exports diesel, petrol, ATF.
Statement 2 is CORRECT: India's refining capacity (~250 MTPA) exceeds domestic consumption (~230 MTPA).
UPSC PRELIMS 2021
Consider the following statements about OPEC+:
1. OPEC+ includes all OPEC members plus G20 oil producers
2. Russia is the key non-OPEC member in OPEC+
Select the correct answer:
(a) 1 only   (b) 2 only   (c) Both   (d) Neither
Answer: (b) 2 only
Statement 1 is WRONG: OPEC+ includes OPEC + select non-OPEC oil exporters (Russia, Kazakhstan, Mexico, etc.) — NOT all G20 oil producers.
Statement 2 is CORRECT: Russia is by far the most important non-OPEC member of OPEC+.
UPSC PRELIMS 2018
Which one of the following is NOT a petroleum product?
(a) Kerosene   (b) Naphtha   (c) Molasses   (d) Asphalt
Answer: (c) Molasses
Molasses is a byproduct of sugar manufacturing — not from crude oil. Kerosene, Naphtha, and Asphalt are all crude oil fractions from the distillation tower.
UPSC PRELIMS 2022
The term "HELP" in the context of India's petroleum sector refers to:
(a) Hydrocarbon Exploration and Licensing Policy
(b) High Energy Licensing Programme
(c) Hydrocarbon Exploration and Leasing Plan
(d) High-yield Exploration Licensing Process
Answer: (a) Hydrocarbon Exploration and Licensing Policy
HELP (2016) replaced NELP. Key features: single licence for all hydrocarbons, revenue sharing contracts, open acreage licensing (OALP), 100% FDI allowed.
UPSC PRELIMS 2023
With reference to the Strait of Hormuz, which of the following is correct?
(a) It connects the Persian Gulf to the Red Sea
(b) It separates Iran from Saudi Arabia
(c) About one-fifth of global oil passes through it
(d) It is controlled by joint US-UAE naval forces
Answer: (c) About one-fifth of global oil passes through it
Hormuz connects the Persian Gulf to the Gulf of Oman (Arabian Sea), NOT Red Sea. It separates Iran (north) from Oman/UAE (south). ~20-21 million barrels/day (~20% global) pass through it.
UPSC PRELIMS 2020
What is the correct sequence of the following in terms of world oil reserves (highest to lowest)?
(a) Saudi Arabia, Venezuela, Iran, Canada
(b) Venezuela, Saudi Arabia, Canada, Iran
(c) Saudi Arabia, Iran, Venezuela, Canada
(d) Venezuela, Saudi Arabia, Iran, Canada
Answer: (b) Venezuela → Saudi Arabia → Canada → Iran
Use the mnemonic "Very Smart Countries Invest" → Venezuela(303Bn) → Saudi(267Bn) → Canada(170Bn) → Iran(158Bn). Venezuela overtook Saudi Arabia in 2011 with reclassification of Orinoco heavy oil.
