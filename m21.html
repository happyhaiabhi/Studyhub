MODULE 21 · FINAL·Modern Bihar

Formation of
Modern Bihar

From a demand raised in 1889 by one man in Arrah to a province in 1912, to Bihar-Orissa in 1912, to Bihar alone in 1936, to an independent state in 1947, to Jharkhand's separation in 2000 — the territory called Bihar has been shaped, reshaped, and redefined across 111 years.

BPSC Special Mains GS-I ⚠ Trap: Bihar Province = 1 April 1912. Bihar separate statehood = 1936. First Council session = 20 Jan 1913 at Bankipore. Jharkhand = 15 Nov 2000 (45.85% of Bihar's land).
🎭Story Hook

The year is 1889. A young lawyer from Arrah named Sachchidananda Sinha stands up at a meeting and says something nobody in power wants to hear: Bihar should be a separate province from Bengal.

He is ignored. He tries again. He starts a journal — Bihar Times (1894). He petitions. He lobbies. He waits. For 23 years, he waits. Then, in 1911, the Hardinge Dispatch finally recommends separation. On 1 April 1912, the province of Bihar and Orissa is formally created.

But the story doesn't end there. In 1936, Bihar and Orissa are separated. In 1947, Bihar becomes part of independent India. In 2000, the tribal heartland becomes Jharkhand, taking away 45.85% of Bihar's land, 18 districts, and a whole civilisation.

Bihar has been reformed three times since 1912. Each time it lost territory — but each time, the Bihar that remained became more defined, more focused, more itself.

💡 Bihar Diwas is celebrated on 22 March each year — but Bihar Province was actually created on 1 April 1912. The March 22 date marks Bihar's contribution to the independence movement; the province formation is April 1.

1889Sachchidananda Sinha First Demands Separate Bihar
Dr Sachchidananda Sinha (from Arrah, Bhojpur) first raised voice for a separate Bihar province in 1889. In 1894, started Bihar Times journal to advocate for separate Bihar. Participated in Home Rule League. He is the father of the idea of Bihar as a separate administrative unit.
1911Hardinge Dispatch — Recommends Separation from Bengal
The Hardinge Dispatch of 1911 recommended the separation of Bihar from Bengal on account of cultural and linguistic differences. This was the official turning point after decades of advocacy by Bihar's leaders.
1 April 1912 ⚠Bihar and Orissa Province Created
Bihar and Orissa Province formally created on 1 April 1912 by the British Government.
  • Legislative Council formed: 43 members total — 24 elected + 19 nominated
  • Capital: Patna
  • First sitting of Council: 20 January 1913 at Bankipore (Patna)
  • Same year (1912): 28th Congress session held at Bankipur under RN Mudholkar
1920First Governor of Bihar and Orissa — Satyendra Prasanna Sinha
Satyendra Prasanna Sinha became the first Governor of Bihar and Orissa in 1920 under the Montague-Chelmsford Reforms. Also first Indian to be a member of the House of Lords and Privy Council.
1936 ⚠Bihar and Orissa Separated — Bihar Gets Statehood
The Government of India Act 1935 introduced reforms. Key outcomes for Bihar:
  • Bihar and Orissa separated as two provinces in 1936 → Bihar attained statehood
  • General elections held in Bihar — Congress won majority (98/107 seats contested)
  • 24 March 1937: Sri Krishna Singh invited by Governor MG Hellet — declined
  • Mohammad Yunus → first PM of Bihar; Shri Krishna Singh cabinet: 20 Jul 1937
15 August 1947India's Independence — Bihar Becomes State of India
Bihar maintained separate existence at Independence. First Governor of Independent Bihar: Jairamdas Daulatram. First CM: Shri Krishna Singh. First Dy CM: Anugrah Narayan Sinha.
1952First General Elections — Bihar Gets Bicameral Legislature
  • First general elections held in Bihar in 1952
  • Total Vidhan Sabha strength: 331 (including 1 nominated)
  • Bihar has bicameral legislature: Vidhan Sabha + Vidhan Parishad
  • First CM: Shri Krishna Singh; Dy CM: Anugrah Narayan Sinha
1 Nov 1956State Reorganisation Act — Bihar Loses Territory to West Bengal
State Reorganisation Commission (1956): 3166 sq miles transferred from Bihar to West Bengal. Kishanganj remained part of Bihar. Vidhan Sabha reduced from 331 to 318 members. In 1977, Delimitation Commission enhanced members from 318 to 324.
15 November 2000 ⚠Jharkhand Carved Out — Bihar's Second Division
Bihar Reorganisation Act 2000 created the state of Jharkhand on 15 November 2000:
  • Land transferred: 45.85% of Bihar's area
  • Districts transferred to Jharkhand: 18 districts
  • Districts remaining with Bihar: 37 districts
  • Vidhan Sabha: reduced from 324 to 243 members
  • Lok Sabha seats: reduced from 54 to 40
  • Vidhan Parishad: reduced from 96 to 75 members

📝PYQ Practice
BPSC 2001
When did the British Government create a new Province of Bihar and Orissa?
(a) 1st April, 1912
(b) 2nd March, 1920
(c) 3rd April, 1912
(d) None of these
✓ CORRECT: (a) 1st April, 1912

The British Government created the Province of Bihar and Orissa on 1 April 1912. The first sitting of the Legislative Council was held on 20 January 1913 at Bankipore (Patna). The Council had 43 members — 24 elected and 19 nominated. The same year, the 28th Congress session was held at Bankipur under RN Mudholkar.

BPSC 2001
The percentage of land area of old Bihar that forms the state of Jharkhand is:
(a) 38.40
(b) 45.85
(c) 42.35
(d) 51.72
✓ CORRECT: (b) 45.85

According to the Bihar Reorganisation Act 2000, 45.85% of Bihar's land area was transferred to the newly formed state of Jharkhand (created 15 November 2000). A total of 18 districts were transferred to Jharkhand. After the division, 37 districts remained with Bihar and the Vidhan Sabha strength was reduced from 324 to 243.

BPSC 2018
Bihar as an Indian state was formed in:
(a) 1911
(b) 1912
(c) 1936
(d) 2000
✓ CORRECT: (c) 1936

⚠️ Critical distinction: Bihar Province was created in 1912 (Bihar + Orissa). Bihar as a separate, independent state (statehood) was achieved in 1936 when Bihar and Orissa were separated under the Government of India Act 1935. Option (b) 1912 = province creation (with Orissa). Option (c) 1936 = Bihar alone as state.

✅Concept Check
1. The first sitting of the Bihar Legislative Council was convened at:
✓ Correct! The first sitting of the Bihar Legislative Council was convened on 20 January 1913 at Bankipore (Patna). This was after the Bihar + Orissa Province was created on 1 April 1912. Patna was the capital. The same area also hosted the 28th Congress session (1912) under RN Mudholkar.
✗ Answer is (C) Bankipore (Patna). First sitting = 20 January 1913 at Bankipore. The Bihar + Orissa Province had been created on 1 April 1912 with Patna as capital.
2. After the formation of Jharkhand (2000), how many districts were left with Bihar?
✓ Correct! After Jharkhand was carved out on 15 November 2000, 37 districts remained with Bihar. 18 districts were transferred to Jharkhand. The total land transferred was 45.85% of Bihar's area. Vidhan Sabha went from 324 to 243; Lok Sabha from 54 to 40; Vidhan Parishad from 96 to 75.
✗ Answer is (C) 37 districts. After Jharkhand took 18 districts, Bihar retained 37. Remember the key numbers: 18 (to Jharkhand) + 37 (Bihar) = 55 total. 45.85% land transferred. Vidhan Sabha 324→243.
MAINS GS-I · 150 WORDS
Trace the territorial evolution of Bihar from 1912 to 2000. What were the key milestones in its formation as a modern state?
Introduction

Bihar's territorial evolution spans over a century (1889–2000), marked by progressive separation from Bengal, from Orissa, and eventually the formation of Jharkhand — each change reshaping what "Bihar" means as a political and cultural entity.

Key Milestones
  • 1889: Sachchidananda Sinha first demands separate Bihar; starts Bihar Times (1894)
  • 1911: Hardinge Dispatch recommends Bihar-Bengal separation
  • 1 April 1912: Bihar and Orissa Province created (43-member Council; Patna = capital)
  • 1920: Satyendra Prasanna Sinha = first Governor of Bihar and Orissa
  • 1936: Bihar separated from Orissa under the Government of India Act 1935 — Bihar attains statehood
  • 1947: Bihar becomes state of independent India; first CM = Shri Krishna Singh
  • 1952: First general elections; bicameral legislature (Vidhan Sabha 331 seats)
  • 1956: State Reorganisation — 3166 sq miles to West Bengal; Vidhan Sabha reduced to 318
  • 2000: Jharkhand created (15 Nov); 18 districts, 45.85% land transferred; Vidhan Sabha 324→243
Conclusion

Bihar's formation is a story of patient assertion — from a lone voice in 1889 to full statehood in 1936 to democratic Bihar in 1952. Each territorial change (1912, 1936, 2000) redefined Bihar's boundaries but not its identity. Today's Bihar of 243 constituencies retains the cultural, historical, and civilisational core that has made it India's most historically significant state since the age of the Buddha.
