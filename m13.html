MODULE 13·Medieval Bihar

Mughal Rule in Bihar —
Province of the Empire

From Akbar's formal annexation in 1576 to the last Mughal governor's dismissal in 1733 — Bihar served as a wealthy subah for 157 years. This module covers each Mughal emperor's reign in Bihar, key governors, and crucial facts tested repeatedly in BPSC.

BPSC Special Mains GS-I ⚠ Trap: Akbar made Bihar subah 1576 (NOT 1556). First Mughal coronated in Patna = Farrukhsiyar (1713). Patna → Azimabad = Aurangzeb's grandson Azimushan (1704).
🎭Story HookSTART HERE

The year is 1704 AD. A Mughal prince, grandson of the great Aurangzeb, arrives in the ancient city of Patna. He looks at this city — founded by Udayin in 455 BC, made capital by Chandragupta Maurya, rebuilt by Sher Shah Suri — and does something audacious: he renames it.

From this day, Patna will be called "Azimabad" — named after himself, Prince Azimushan (Azim-us-Shan). This name appears on coins, in court records, in farman letters. And yet — Azimabad never stuck. The city remembered its ancient name and would answer to nothing else. Today we still call it Patna.

This small act of naming tells the whole story of Mughal Bihar: powerful enough to rename an ancient city, but never powerful enough to actually change it.

💡 Fun Fact: Patna has been renamed twice in history — by Udayin (from various pre-existing names to Pataliputra), and by Azimushan to Azimabad (1704). Both times, the city reverted to Patna. Some names are simply permanent.
🧠Mughal Bihar — Emperor by EmperorWITH ANALOGY
🏛️ Modern Analogy

Think of Mughal Bihar like a valuable state in a federal government. The Centre (Delhi) was very interested in Bihar's revenue (saltpetre, opium, cotton, silk were valuable). They kept rotating governors (like bureaucratic transfers). Some governors were excellent (Man Singh, Saif Khan); some were terrible. As the Mughal centre weakened after Aurangzeb, Bihar began slipping toward independence — which became the Nawabs of Bengal in 1733.

Akbar and Bihar
1556–1605 AD
1576 — Bihar made Subah ⚠ After defeating Daud Khan Kararani at Battle of Rajmahal (July 1576), Akbar made Bihar a separate Subah of his empire. First governor: Munim Khan. ⚠ Bihar made subah in 1576, NOT 1556 (accession year).
Man Singh (1587–1594) Appointed 17 March 1587. Made Rohtas (Rohtasgarh fort) his capital. Renovated Rohtasgarh Fort. Defeated Bhojpur, Giddhaur, Kharagpur. Per Abul Fazl: "administered excellently and crushed all rebellions." Transferred to Bengal 1594.
Mithila — Mahesh Thakur 1577 AD — Akbar appointed Mahesh Thakur as administrator of Mithila. Made Rajnagar (Madhubani) his capital.
Asif Khan Last Governor of Bihar under Akbar. After Akbar's death 1605, replaced by Jahangir's first appointee.
Jahangir and Bihar
1605–1627 AD
Lala Beg (Beg Bahadur) Replaced Asif Khan. Suppressed revolt of Sangram Singh of Kharagpur → given title Jahangir Quli. During this period: Nur Jahan (widow of Sher Afghan) passed through Darbhanga → built Noor-sarai and mosque there.
Ibrahim Khan Brother of Nur Jahan. Conquered Kukradesh (Chotanagpur) and acquired diamond mines. Arrested Durjan Sal; sent to Jahangir's court (imprisoned in Gwalior). Promoted → Governor of Bengal (1617).
Prince Parvez (1621) First Mughal Prince to become Governor of Bihar. Jahangir declared him governor 1621. Gave his name to Parvezabad (now known as Paleza). His officer Nazar Bahadur built Pathar-ki-Masjid (Sangi Masjid) in Patna.
Shah Jahan and Bihar
1627–1658 AD · Peaceful era
Khan-i-Alam First governor; inefficient; recalled within a year.
Saif Khan (Mirza Safi) Best governor of Bihar under Mughals (per Peter Mundy, 1632). Built Shahi Idgah in Patna. Built madrasa and mosque. Two intellectuals from Tirhut sent to imperial court — ancestors of Raja of Darbhanga.
Jafar Khan (1651) Built famous Bagh-i-Jafar Khan at Patna + single-domed Dundi Bazar mosque.
Aurangzeb and Bihar
1658–1707 AD
Daud Khan Quraishi First governor under Aurangzeb. Defeated Cheros of Palamu → extended empire to Chotanagpur. Founded Daudnagar (Gaya district).
Jan Nisar Khan (Lashkar Khan) During his tenure: European travellers Tavernier and Bernier visited Patna.
Azimushan (1702–1704) ⚠ Aurangzeb's grandson. Made Subedar of Bihar 1702. Came to Patna 1703–04. Renamed Patna as "Azimabad" in 1704 AD — after himself. ⚠ KEY BPSC FACT: Patna → Azimabad = Azimushan, 1704.
Post-Aurangzeb Bihar (1707–1733)
Mughal decline · Nawabs of Bengal take over
Farrukhsiyar (1713) ⚠ First Mughal ruler crowned/coronated in Patna (1713). ⚠ KEY BPSC FACT. Under his rule: 4 Subedars — Kairat Khan, Mir Jumla, Buland Khan, Khan Jaman.
Fakr-ud-daula ⚠ Last Mughal Governor of Bihar. Appointed during reign of Muhammad Shah (1719–1748). Dismissed in 1733 by the Nawab of Bengal. Built "Court of Justice" at Patna during his tenure.
After 1733 Nawabs of Bengal (Alivardi Khan) established control over Bihar. Mughal era in Bihar effectively ended. Bihar passed to Nawab of Bengal's administration — setting stage for European entry (Module 15).

📝PYQ PracticeSOLVED
BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2008
Akbar merged Bihar and Bengal into the Mughal empire in:
(a) 1560
(b) 1575
(c) 1576
(d) 1572
✓ CORRECT: (c) 1576

Akbar made Bihar a separate Subah (province) of his empire in 1576 AD, after defeating Daud Khan Kararani at the Battle of Rajmahal in July 1576. ⚠ Don't confuse with 1556 (Akbar's accession year) or 1575 (Battle of Tukaroi where Daud was captured). The formal province creation was 1576. First governor: Munim Khan.

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2005
Who was the last Mughal Governor of Bihar?
(a) Kairat Khan
(b) Mir Jumla
(c) Buland Khan
(d) Fakr-ud-daula
✓ CORRECT: (d) Fakr-ud-daula

Fakr-ud-daula was the last Mughal Governor of Bihar. He was appointed during the reign of Muhammad Shah (1719–1748) and dismissed in 1733 AD by the Nawab of Bengal. Options (a), (b), (c) — Kairat Khan, Mir Jumla, Buland Khan — were the four subedars appointed under Farrukhsiyar, not the last governor. After Fakr-ud-daula, Bihar came under the Nawabs of Bengal.

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2015
Which Sikh Guru was born in Patna?
(a) Guru Nanak
(b) Guru Teg Bahadur
(c) Guru Hargobind
(d) Guru Gobind Singh
✓ CORRECT: (d) Guru Gobind Singh

Guru Gobind Singh, the 10th and last Sikh Guru, was born at Patna Sahib, Patna in 1666 AD. His father Guru Teg Bahadur was on a peace mission to Assam and had left his wife at Patna, where she gave birth. Guru Nanak had visited Patna (1509) but was NOT born there. Guru Teg Bahadur passed through Patna but was NOT born there. Takht Shri Harmandir Saheb (Patna Sahib) = birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh = one of 5 Takhts of Sikhism.

✅Concept CheckTEST YOURSELF
1. Who was the first Mughal ruler to be sworn in (coronated) at Patna?
✓ Correct! Farrukhsiyar was the first Mughal emperor to be coronated (sworn in) at Patna in 1713. This is a standard BPSC fact. He was the grandson of Bahadur Shah I (Shah Alam I). During his reign, 4 subedars were appointed in Bihar: Kairat Khan, Mir Jumla, Buland Khan, and Khan Jaman.
✗ Answer is (B) Farrukhsiyar. He was coronated at Patna in 1713 — the only Mughal emperor to be sworn in at Patna (not Delhi). This is a frequently tested BPSC fact that students often miss.
2. Aurangzeb had made his grandson Azimushan as Subedar of Bihar in which year?
✓ Correct! Aurangzeb appointed his grandson Azimushan (Prince Azim) as Subedar of Bihar in 1702 AD. Azimushan came to Patna in 1703–04 and renamed it Azimabad in 1704 AD. Note the distinction: appointed = 1702; came to Patna + renamed = 1703–1704.
✗ Answer is (B) 1702. Aurangzeb appointed Azimushan as Bihar Subedar in 1702 AD. He came to Patna in 1703–04 and renamed it Azimabad in 1704. Option (C) 1704 = year he renamed Patna, not year he was appointed.
MAINS GS-I · 150 WORDS
Describe Bihar's position as a Mughal province from 1576 to 1733. Which governor made the most significant contribution to its administration?
Introduction

Bihar served as a Mughal Subah for 157 years (1576–1733), valued for its agricultural wealth, saltpetre production, and strategic position between Agra/Delhi and Bengal.

Bihar's Strategic Importance

Bihar supplied saltpetre (for gunpowder), opium, cotton, and silk to the Mughal empire. Its Ganga waterways made it crucial for troop movement. Control of Bihar meant control of the eastern corridor. British, Dutch, French and Danish traders were all attracted by Bihar's commodities.

Best Governor: Man Singh (1587–1594)
  • Appointed 17 March 1587 by Akbar; transferred 1594
  • Made Rohtas (Rohtasgarh Fort) his administrative capital; renovated the fort
  • Suppressed rebellions in Bhojpur, Giddhaur, Kharagpur
  • Per Abul Fazl: "administered excellently and crushed all rebellions"
Key Turning Points
  • 1576: Akbar's annexation after Battle of Rajmahal
  • 1704: Azimushan renames Patna as Azimabad (name rejected by history)
  • 1713: Farrukhsiyar crowned at Patna — rare honor for the province
  • 1733: Fakr-ud-daula dismissed — Mughal era ends
Conclusion

The Mughal era gave Bihar 157 years of relative administrative stability under rotating governors. However, as Mughal power weakened after Aurangzeb, Bihar's governance fell first to the Nawabs of Bengal (1733) and then to the East India Company — a transition that would define Bihar's modern history.
