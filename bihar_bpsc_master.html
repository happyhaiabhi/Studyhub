Historical Scenario

Revolts, movements, personalities & organisations in Bihar's history

🏛️ Origin of Bihar & Bihar Sharif

The word Bihar derives from "Vihara" (Buddhist monastery). The region was filled with Buddhist monasteries. Bihar Sharif served as a centre of education, trade, administration and religion in medieval times. Odantapuri University was established here by Pala Emperor Gopala. The Sufi saint Sharfuddin Yahya Maneri (Firdausi order, 1262–1380 AD) is buried here — famous work: Maktoobat-e-Sadi.

🏙️ Patna (Pataliputra) — Historical Capital

Founded byAjatashatru (Haryanka dynasty) Made capital byUdayin (Ajatashatru's successor) Dynasties ruled from hereHaryankaShishunagaNandaMauryaShungaGupta Medieval capitalSher Shah Suri rebuilt Patna as capital in 1541 AD (from Tarikh-i-Daudi)

⚔️ First Muslim Conquest of Bihar

Bakhtiyar Khalji was Bihar's first Muslim conqueror. His invasion destroyed the great Buddhist universities of Odantpuri, Nalanda and Vikramshila, effectively ending Buddhist scholarship in India. Most Asked

🌿 Tribal Revolts

Santhal Rebellion (1855–56): Santhals declared their government in Bhagalpur–Rajmahal region (Daman-e-Koh). Leaders: Sidhu, Kanhu, Chand, Bhairava. Assembly of 10,000+ at Bhognadih village on 30 June 1855.

Munda Rebellion / Ulgulan (1899–1900): Led by Birsa Munda (born 1875, Ulihatu village, Khunti). Mentor: Anand Pandey. Captured at Chakradharpur, 3 February 1900.

Chhotanagpur Tenancy Act 1908: Abolished Beth-Begari (forced labour), protected Khutkatti rights of tribals.

🚫 Civil Disobedience & Quit India

  • Salt Satyagraha 1930: Bihar farmers refused Chowkidari Tax (village watchmen) — uniquely Bihar's contribution
  • August Revolution 1942: Linlithgow ordered firing on Patna mobs on 11 August 1942. 7 students killed near Bihar Legislative Assembly.
  • Non-Cooperation 1920–22: JP Narayan left Patna College with 20 days to go for exams; joined Bihar Vidyapeeth.

Bihar's Formation

Key dates, acts, governors, districts & institutions

📅 Key Dates of Bihar's Formation

1733 ADBihar merged into Bengal province under Nawab Shuja-ud-Daula 12 Dec 1911Demand accepted at Imperial Court, Delhi — Bihar to be carved from Bengal 22 Mar 1912Bihar Diwas — Bihar + Odisha province proclaimed (separated from Bengal Presidency) 1 Apr 1912Bihar & Odisha province came into existence with Patna as capital 1 Apr 1936Odisha separated from Bihar (under Govt. of India Act 1935) 15 Nov 2000Jharkhand carved out of Bihar as 28th state; Ranchi as capital

Bihar Diwas 22 March every year — 2024 was 112th Foundation Day.

📜 Legal Acts Governing Bihar

  • Indian Councils Act 1861 (amended 1912): Bihar Legislative Council formed
  • First sitting of Bihar Legislative Council: 20 January 1913, Bankipore
  • Govt. of India Act 1935: Bihar became a fully separate province

🗂️ Districts: Then and Now

  • End of 1995: 55 districts in Bihar
  • After Jharkhand formed (Nov 2000): 37 districts left
  • After Arwal district created: 38 districts currently
  • Jharkhand comprises 45.84% of old Bihar's area (79,714 sq km)
  • Current Bihar area: 94,163 sq km (2.86% of India) — 12th largest state

Geographical Scenario

Location, rivers, hills, climate, soils & agro-climatic zones

🌍 Location & Basics

Longitude83°19'50"E to 88°17'40"E Area94,163 sq km — 12th largest state, 2.86% of India Climate (Köppen)Cwg — Monsoon with dry winter Standard TimeSame as IST (82°30'E longitude) BordersUP (8 districts), Jharkhand (8 districts), West Bengal, Nepal

🏔️ Physical Features

  • Someshwar Hills — NW Bihar (West Champaran); youngest (Pleistocene age); part of Shivalik ranges
  • Kaimur Plateau — famous for Limestone; part of Vindhyan range
  • Kharagpur Hills — geologically oldest (Dharwar range)
  • Rajgir — surrounded by 5 hills: Ratnagiri, Vipalachal, Vaibhagiri, Songiri, Udaygiri
  • Brahmayoni Hill — Gaya district; mentioned in Mahabharata & Buddhist records
  • Rohtas district altitude: 107.78 metres above sea level

🌊 Rivers of Bihar

Main riverGanga — longest stretch in Patna district (~99 km) North-bank tributariesGhaghra, Gandak, Burhi-Gandak, Kamla-Balan, Bagmati, Kosi, Mahananda South-bank tributariesKarmnasa, Son, Punpun, Kiul-Harohar, Badua, Chandan Falgu RiverAncient name: Niranjana. ☁️ Climate & Rainfall

  • Tarai region: parallel to India–Nepal border (7 districts: W.Champaran, E.Champaran, Sitamarhi, Madhubani, Supaul, Araria, Kishanganj)

  • Bihar Silk Industry — self-employment to over 1,50,000 people
