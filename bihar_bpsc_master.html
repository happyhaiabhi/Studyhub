Introduction to Bihar

Overview, location, significance, and basic profile

📍 Bihar: Location & Overview

Name Origin: Derived from "Vihara" (Buddhist monasteries)

Location: Eastern India; bordered by UP, Jharkhand, West Bengal, and Nepal

Area: 94,163 sq km — 12th largest state, 2.86% of India

Capital: Patna

Bihar Diwas: 22 March — celebrates separation from Bengal (1912)

🌍 Key Statistics

Population~104 million (2011 Census) Literacy Rate~63.8% Density~1,102 per sq km Sex Ratio~918 per 1000 males
02 / 08

History of Bihar

Ancient, medieval, colonial periods and freedom movement

⚔️ Ancient Bihar & Dynasties

  • Pataliputra (Patna): Founded by Ajatashatru; capital under Udayin
  • Mauryan Empire (322-185 BC): Ashoka — greatest emperor
  • Gupta Period (320-550 AD): Golden age; Samudragupta, Chandragupta II

🕌 Medieval Period

Bakhtiyar Khalji (1193 AD): Destroyed Nalanda, Odantpuri, Vikramshila

Sher Shah Suri: Rebuilt Patna as capital in 1541 AD

🔥 Revolts & Independence

Kunwar Singh: 1857 Revolt hero; Zamindar of Jagdishpur

Champaran Satyagraha (1917): Gandhi's campaign against indigo taxation

03 / 08

Geography of Bihar

Physical features, rivers, climate, soils, and minerals

🌍 Location & Area

Latitude23°48'N to 28°2'N Longitude83°19'50"E to 88°17'40"E Area94,163 sq km

⛰️ Physical Features

  • Someshwar Hills: NW Bihar; Shivalik range
  • Kaimur Plateau: Famous for limestone
  • Rajgir: Surrounded by 5 hills

🌊 Rivers

Ganga: Main river, longest stretch in Patna district (~99 km)

Tributaries: Gandak, Kosi, Son, Karmnasa, Falgu

⛏️ Minerals

Pyrite: Bihar has monopoly in India

Gold: Largest reserves in Jamui district

Limestone: Kaimur, Munger, Rohtas

Economy of Bihar

Agriculture, industries, energy, and economic development

🌾 Agriculture & Crops

Main Cash Crop: Sugarcane

Specialty Crops: Litchi (45% of India), Makhana (90%)

Pulses: Moong, Masoor (lentil)

🏭 Industries

  • Sugar Industry: West Champaran
  • Silk: Bhagalpur; 150,000+ artisans
  • Cement: Based on limestone

📊 Economic Data

Growth Rate 2022-23: 10.6%

Agriculture Share: 47.8% population
