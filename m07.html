MODULE 07·Ancient Bihar — Final

Post-Mauryan to
Gupta Era

From Pushyamitra Shunga's dramatic coup in 185 BC to the golden age of Gupta emperors — Bihar witnessed India's cultural renaissance, the founding of Nalanda University, and the birth of Aryabhatta's mathematics. This module closes the ancient story of Bihar.

BPSC Special Mains GS-I 185 BC – 550 AD ⚠ Trap: Nalanda founded by Kumargupta I (NOT Chandragupta II); Napoleon of India = Samudragupta
🎭Story HookSTART HERE

The year is 185 BC. The last Mauryan emperor, old and feeble Brihadratha, is reviewing his troops. His commander-in-chief, a brilliant Brahmin general named Pushyamitra Shunga, steps forward — and kills him in front of the entire army. No one protests. The soldiers hail Pushyamitra as their new king.

With that single act, India's first great empire — the Maurya dynasty — ended. But Bihar did not fade. Over the next 650 years, it would see five more dynasties, survive Greek invasions, produce India's greatest mathematician Aryabhatta, and give the world the Nalanda University — the ancient world's greatest centre of learning, where 10,000 students from 20 countries studied simultaneously.

This is the story of Bihar between empires — not a dark age, but a golden one.

💡 Bihar gave the world Nalanda (427–1197 AD) — 770 years of continuous scholarship, longer than any modern university's existence.
🧠Core Concept — Five Dynasties in OrderWITH ANALOGY
🏃 Modern Analogy

Think of these 650 years as Bihar's "post-independence era before the next superpower." After the Maurya "British Raj" ended, smaller governments ran Bihar (Shunga, Kanva, Kushana) — each contributing something — until the Gupta dynasty arose as independent India's first government, bringing a true golden age of art, science, and literature. Nalanda was their IIT.

184 BC – 72 BC
1. Shunga Dynasty
BRAHMANICAL REVIVAL
Pushyamitra Shunga 185–148 BC Founder — killed last Mauryan Brihadratha. Was Commander-in-Chief of Mauryan army. Ruled Magadha, Kosala, Sakala, Malwa, Berar. Staunch Brahmanist. Conducted 2 Ashwamedha Yajnas (confirmed by Ayodhya inscription of Dhandev). Patanjali (author of Mahabhashya) was chief priest. Renovated Buddhist monuments at Bharhut and Sanchi — despite being depicted as anti-Buddhist by some texts.
Agnimitra 148–140 BC Son of Pushyamitra. Hero of Kalidasa's drama Malavikagnimitram. Ruled from Vidisha.
Devbhuti Last ruler 10th and last ruler of Shunga dynasty. Overthrown by Vasudeva Kanva in 72 BC. Greeks (Bactrians) influenced this period — Heliodorus erected Garuda pillar at Bernagar during Shunga rule.
72 BC – 27 BC
2. Kanva Dynasty
SHORT-LIVED
Vasudeva 72–66 BC (Founder) Overthrew last Shunga ruler Devbhuti in 72 BC. Founded Kanva dynasty. Ruled for only 45 years total across 4 rulers.
Susharman Last ruler ~27 BC Last Kanva ruler. Kanva rule ended when Satavahanas from Deccan rose to power. However, no direct Satavahana rule over Bihar — region entered a period of fragmentation.
1st–3rd Century AD
3. Kushana Dynasty
CENTRAL ASIAN
Kanishka 78–125 AD Attacked Patliputra. Took Buddhist monk Asvaghosha (author of Buddhacharita) from Patliputra to his court. Asvaghosha = Mahayana Buddhism's great philosopher, born at Saket (Ayodhya). Kushana coins found at Chirand (Saran), Buxar, Bodh Gaya, Vaishali, Kumrahar. 4th Buddhist Council held under Kanishka in Kashmir — Mahayana Buddhism emerged.
After Kushana decline 3rd C AD Licchavis ruled the region after Kushana decline. Period of unrest until Guptas emerged in 3rd century AD. Kushana decline → power vacuum → Gupta opportunity.
275 AD – 550 AD
4. Gupta Empire — The Golden Age
⭐ MOST IMPORTANT
Srigupta 275–280 AD (Founder) First Gupta king. Ruled small portion of N. Bengal and S. Bihar. Subordinate to later Kushanas. Succeeded by son Ghatotkacha (280–319 AD).
Chandragupta I 319–335 AD First independent Gupta ruler. First to use title Maharajadhiraj. Started Gupta Era (319–320 AD). Married Licchavi princess Kumardevi — great political coup. Issued gold coins commemorating this marriage. Son Samudragupta called himself Lichchavi-dauhitra (son-in-law of Licchavis).
Samudragupta 335–380 AD "Napoleon of India" — title given by Vincent Arthur Smith. Prayag Prashasti (inscription) written by Harisena, discovered by Joseph Tiefenthaler — describes his conquests in Brahmi script, Sanskrit. "Exterminator of all kings" (Mathura stone inscription). Patron of arts: title Kaviraja. Played Veena (shown on gold coins). Harisena and Vasubandhu adorned his court. Allowed Ceylon king Meghavarman to build monastery at Bodh Gaya. Extended from Himalayas to Narmada.
Chandragupta II 'Vikramaditya' 380–415 AD Killed brother Ramagupta; married his widow Dhruvadevi. Conquered Mathura (from Kushanas), Gujarat (from Shakas). First Gupta to issue gold coins. Title: Vikramaditya, Simhavikrama. Ujjain = second capital. Nine Ratnas in court: Kalidasa, Varahmihira, Dhanvantari (physician), Varruchi, Amarsimha, Shanku, Vetalbhatta, Kshapanaka, Ghatakarpara. Fa-Hien visited during his reign (399–412 AD).
Kumargupta I (Mahendraditya) 415–455 AD Founded Nalanda University (427 AD). This is a KEY trap — not Chandragupta II. Nalanda functioned 427–1197 AD (770 years). Hiuen Tsang studied here. Worshipped Kartikeya — coins show Kartikeya. First Huna attack in his reign.
Skandagupta 455–467 AD Last great Gupta ruler. Repulsed Huna attack (455 AD). Bhitari inscription records this. Junagarh inscription: Governor restored dam on Sudarshana lake (originally built by Chandragupta Maurya). After Skandagupta's death, Gupta empire disintegrated. Last Gupta ruler: Vishnugupta.
⏳Master Timeline — 185 BC to 550 ADCHRONOLOGICAL
185 BC
Pushyamitra Shunga Kills Last Mauryan — Brahmanical Revival Begins
Coup in front of the army. Shunga dynasty begins. Brahminism revives; two Ashwamedha Yajnas conducted. Despite anti-Buddhist portrayal, renovated Bharhut and Sanchi stupas.
72 BC
Kanva Dynasty Founded — Brief Interlude (72–27 BC)
Vasudeva Kanva overthrows last Shunga ruler Devbhuti. Only 4 rulers in 45 years. Satavahanas rise in south but don't directly rule Bihar.
78–125 AD — KUSHANA KANISHKA
Kanishka Attacks Patliputra — Takes Asvaghosha
Central Asian Kushanas extend into Bihar. Kanishka attacks Patliputra, takes Buddhist philosopher Asvaghosha. 4th Buddhist Council in Kashmir — Mahayana Buddhism emerges. Coins found at Chirand, Buxar, Bodh Gaya, Vaishali.
275 AD — GUPTA ERA BEGINS
Srigupta Founds Gupta Dynasty
Small kingdom in N. Bengal/S. Bihar. Subordinate to Kushanas initially. Son Ghatotkacha follows (280–319 AD). Foundation of India's second great empire laid in Bihar.
319–320 AD
Chandragupta I — Gupta Era Proclaimed Maharajadhiraj
First independent Gupta ruler. Married Licchavi princess Kumardevi — political masterstroke, consolidated power in Bihar. Started Gupta Era (319–320 AD). Title: Maharajadhiraj.
335–380 AD
Samudragupta — "Napoleon of India" Conquers All
Prayag Prashasti inscription by Harisena describes 20+ kingdoms incorporated. Extended from Himalayas to Narmada, Brahmaputra to Yamuna. Title: Kaviraja (patron of arts). Played Veena.
380–415 AD
Chandragupta II Vikramaditya — Peak of Cultural Golden Age
Nine Ratnas including Kalidasa. Fa-Hien visits Patliputra. Conquers Gujarat from Shakas. Issues first Gupta gold coins. Ujjain = second capital. Patliputra and Ujjain = centres of learning.
427 AD ⚠ TRAP
Kumargupta I Founds Nalanda University
NOT Chandragupta II. Kumargupta I (Mahendraditya) establishes Nalanda. Functions 427–1197 AD (770 years). 10,000 students, 1,500 teachers at its peak. Hiuen Tsang studied here (637–644 AD).
455 AD
Skandagupta Repulses Hunas — Last Great Gupta
Bhitari inscription records Huna defeat. Died 467 AD. After him: Purugupta, Buddhagupta, Narasimhagupta, Vishnugupta (last). Hunas continued attacking — empire disintegrated by 550 AD.
🔄Flowchart — Why the Gupta Age Was GoldenCAUSE → EVENT → RESULT
Causes

Conditions That Enabled the Golden Age

Licchavi alliance (Chandragupta I). Decentralised governance (unlike Maurya). Iron trade routes. Peaceful north after Kushana decline.

Events / Achievements

What Made It "Golden"

Samudragupta's conquests. Chandragupta II's Nine Ratnas. Kalidasa's Abhijnanasakuntalam. Aryabhatta's Aryabhattiya. Nalanda University founded. Varahmihira's astronomy.

Results / Legacy

Bihar's Contribution to India

Zero concept (Aryabhatta). Solar system model. Sanskrit classics. Buddhist learning exported worldwide from Nalanda. Art & sculpture renaissance. Gupta temples established Hindu temple architecture.

👤Eminent Ancient Personalities from BiharBPSC SPECIAL

Aryabhatta

Born: Patliputra (ancient Kusumpur) · Gupta Era

Greatest astronomer-mathematician. Stated Earth revolves around Sun and rotates on its axis. First to conceptualise zero. Studied solar/lunar eclipses. Masterwork: Aryabhattiya — composed at Kusumapura (near Patliputra). BPSC always asks: "Aryabhatta related to which place?" → Patliputra.

Asvaghosha

Born: Saket (Ayodhya) · Kushana Era (80 CE)

Greatest Buddhist philosopher-poet of Kushana era. Followed Mahayana Buddhism. Wrote Buddhacharita (Buddha's biography), Saundarananda. Kanishka attacked Patliputra and took him from Varanasi's king. Associated with Bihar through Patliputra connection.

Acharya Chanakya (Kautilya)

Pataliputra · Mauryan Era (321 BC)

Chandragupta Maurya's minister and political genius. Also known as Kautilya or Vishnugupta. Wrote Arthashastra (15 sections/adhikarans) — greatest treatise on governance, economics, warfare. Engineered downfall of Nanda dynasty. His untied topknot vow is one of history's most dramatic pledges.

Buddhaghosh

Morund Khetak village near Bodh Gaya · 5th C AD

Great Buddhist commentator. Famous works: Visuddhimagga (Path of Purification), Sumangal Vilasini, Manorathapurani, Atthasalini. Went to Sri Lanka to spread Buddhism. His work helped preserve Pali Buddhist literature.

Sariputta (Shariputra)

Born near Rajgir (Bihar) · 6th C BC

One of Buddha's two chief disciples. Brilliant Brahmin, master of Vedas — converted to Buddhism and became its greatest philosophical systematiser. Born near Nalanda (Rajgir area). Died before Buddha at Nalanda. The Nalanda site is named partly in his honour.

Panini

Studied at Maner, Patna · 6th C BC

Greatest Sanskrit grammarian. Wrote Ashtadhyayi — the foundational text of Sanskrit grammar with 3,959 rules. Studied grammar at Maner near Patna — Bihar connection. Born in Shalatula (modern Pakistan). His grammar is the basis of all classical Sanskrit literature.

📝PYQ PracticeSOLVED
BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2015
Who was the founder of Nalanda University?
(a) Chandragupta Vikramaditya (CG-II)
(b) Kumargupta-I
(c) Dharampal (Pala king)
(d) Pushyamitra Shunga
✓ CORRECT: (b) Kumargupta I

⚠️ Classic trap: Many students answer Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya) because he was the "greatest" Gupta. But Nalanda was founded by his son Kumargupta I (Mahendraditya) in 427 AD. Nalanda operated from 427 to 1197 AD (770 years) until destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji. Hiuen Tsang (637–644 AD) was its most famous student. Option (c) Dharampal founded Vikramshila University — not Nalanda.

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2011
Who among the following had defeated the Hunas?
(a) Samudragupta
(b) Chandragupta II
(c) Skandagupta
(d) Ramagupta
✓ CORRECT: (c) Skandagupta

Skandagupta (455–467 AD) repulsed the Huna attack in 455 AD — recorded in the Bhitari inscription. He is considered the last great Gupta ruler. Hunas were Central Asian nomads who repeatedly attacked from the northwest. After Skandagupta's death (467 AD), Huna invasions resumed and eventually broke up the Gupta empire by ~550 AD.

BPSC PrelimsBPSC 2015
Aryabhatta, the great astronomer, was related to which place?
(a) Vaishali
(b) Patliputra
(c) Odanpuri
(d) Nalanda
✓ CORRECT: (b) Patliputra

Aryabhatta was born in Patliputra (ancient Kusumpur). His famous work Aryabhattiya was composed at Kusumapura, near Patliputra. He was the first to state that Earth revolves around the Sun, rotates on its axis, and studied solar/lunar eclipses. He is credited with conceptualising the value of zero. Bihar is proud to call him its own.

✅Concept CheckTEST YOURSELF
1. Samudragupta was given the title "Napoleon of India" by which historian?
✓ Correct! Vincent Arthur Smith gave Samudragupta the title "Napoleon of India" based on his extensive military conquests. Joseph Tiefenthaler was the person who discovered the Prayag Prashasti inscription that described Samudragupta's campaigns. Harisena wrote the inscription.
✗ Answer is (B) Vincent Arthur Smith. He coined the phrase "Napoleon of India" for Samudragupta. The Prayag Prashasti inscription (written by court poet Harisena) described Samudragupta as "exterminator of all kings."
2. Gupta period coins have been found from which place in Bihar?
✓ Correct! Gupta period coins were found at Hajipur (Vaishali district). This is a standard Bihar archaeology fact. Rajgir has Mauryan remains; Sasaram has Sher Shah's tomb; Munger has Chalcolithic and Buddhist remains.
✗ Answer is (B) Hajipur. Gupta period coins were found at Hajipur in Vaishali district. This fact from Arihant Bihar History is a direct BPSC question (Q2 of Ancient History MCQs). Don't confuse with Kushana coins (Chirand, Buxar) or Mauryan evidence (Kumhrar).

The Gupta period is considered the "Golden Age" of ancient India. Discuss Bihar's central role in this golden age, with special reference to Nalanda University and the contribution of Aryabhatta.
Introduction

The Gupta period (275–550 AD), headquartered in Pataliputra (modern Patna), is India's acknowledged "Golden Age" — a time of unparalleled achievements in science, literature, philosophy, and art. Bihar was its epicentre.

Bihar as Gupta Heartland

The Guptas originated in south Bihar. Chandragupta I secured Bihar by marrying into the Licchavi clan (of Vaishali). Samudragupta's campaigns radiated from Pataliputra. The empire's greatest cultural achievements were produced here — including Kalidasa's Sanskrit dramas and Varahmihira's astronomical treatises.

Nalanda — The World's First International University
  • Founded by Kumargupta I (427 AD) in Nalanda district, Bihar
  • Functioned for 770 continuous years (427–1197 AD)
  • 10,000 students, 1,500 teachers at its peak
  • Students from China, Korea, Japan, Tibet, Mongolia, Sri Lanka
  • Hiuen Tsang's account (637–644 AD) describes its grandeur
  • Destroyed by Bakhtiyar Khalji (1197 AD)
Aryabhatta — Bihar's Scientific Giant
  • Born in Pataliputra (ancient Kusumpur)
  • First to state Earth revolves around Sun and rotates on its axis
  • Calculated accurate value of π (pi)
  • Conceptualised the value of zero
  • Studied solar/lunar eclipses scientifically
  • Masterwork: Aryabhattiya, written at Kusumapura
Conclusion

Bihar's Gupta-era contributions — Nalanda's international scholarship and Aryabhatta's revolutionary science — represent humanity's highest intellectual achievements of the 1st millennium AD. Modern India's space agency (ISRO) named its first satellite "Aryabhata" in 1975, honouring Bihar's greatest ancient son.
