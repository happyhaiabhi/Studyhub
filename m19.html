MODULE 19·Modern Bihar

Freedom Movements
in Bihar

Bihar's freedom movement was a unique blend of peasant resistance, nationalist politics, and Gandhian satyagraha. From the Champaran Satyagraha (1917) — Gandhi's very first experiment in India — to the Quit India students shot at Patna Assembly (1942), Bihar was always at the heart of the struggle.

BPSC Special Mains GS-I ⚠ Trap: Gandhi arrived Champaran 10 April 1917 (NOT 15 April). Rajkumar Shukla invited Gandhi. Tinkathia abolished 4 March 1918. First Bihar PM = Mohammad Yunus (NOT Shri Krishna Singh).
🎭Story Hook

The year is 10 April 1917. A thin man in a dhoti steps off a train at Motihari, Champaran. The local magistrate orders him to leave immediately. The man refuses. This act of civil disobedience — his first in India — will change the course of the independence movement.

The man is Mahatma Gandhi. The man who brought him there is Rajkumar Shukla, a simple peasant from Champaran who had tracked Gandhi all the way to the 1916 Lucknow Congress session to beg him to come see the indigo farmers' suffering.

When Gandhi arrived, he found farmers forced to grow indigo on 3/20 of their land (Tinkathia system) and pay extortionate rents. His campaign here created India's first successful civil disobedience outcome: the Champaran Agrarian Committee, and on 4 March 1918, the Tinkathia system was abolished. Bihar gave Gandhi his first Indian victory.

💡 Rajkumar Shukla — a semi-literate peasant from Murli Bhitiharwa village, Champaran — is the man who brought Gandhi to Bihar and thereby changed the course of Indian history.
🧠All Major Freedom Movements — Bihar-specific

Early Nationalism & Congress in Bihar

1899–1912
  • Boys' Association inaugurated 3 March 1899 under chairmanship of Afak Khan. Founder: Suryadev Narayan Sharma. Centre of political activities till 1920.
  • BPCC (Bihar Provincial Congress Committee) formed 1908 at Sonepur fair. First session: Patna 1908 under presidentship of Sayed Hasan Imam.
  • 28th Congress session at Bankipur (Patna) — 1912 under RN Mudholkar. Mazharul Haque = Chairman; Sachchidananda Sinha = Secretary. Created new political awakening in Bihar.

Home Rule Movement in Bihar

1916
  • All India Home Rule League formed April 1916 (under Tilak + Annie Besant)
  • Bihar Home Rule League established at Bankipur (Patna) on 16 December 1916
  • President: Mazhar-ul-Haque. Vice-Presidents: Sarfaraz Hussain Khan, Purnendu Narain Sinha

⭐ Champaran Satyagraha — Gandhi's First Indian Satyagraha

1917

Tinkathia System: European planters forced peasants to grow indigo on 3/20 of their total land. High rents + illegal dues. Massive poverty.

  • Rajkumar Shukla (from Murli Bhitiharwa village) invited Gandhi at Lucknow Congress (1916). Shukla urged Gandhi repeatedly to visit Champaran.
  • Gandhi arrived Champaran: 10 April 1917. Stayed at Sant Raut's house in Amolwa village.
  • 11 April 1917: Met Bihar Planters Association minister in Muzaffarpur.
  • 15 April 1917: Leaders joined Gandhi — Rajendra Prasad, Anugrah Narayan Singh, Mazhar-ul-Haque, Mahadeo Desai, Narhari Parikh, JB Kriplani.
  • British constituted Champaran Agrarian Committee (10 June 1917).
  • 4 March 1918: Tinkathia system abolished; rent reduced; farmers compensated.
  • 1 May 1918: Champaran Agrarian Act passed.
  • 13 November 1917: Gandhi established first ever basic school at Barharwain Lakhansen village.

Non-Cooperation Movement in Bihar

1920–1922
  • Bihar Congress provincial meet August 1920 under presidentship of Rajendra Prasad — passed non-cooperation resolution
  • National college established on Patna-Gaya road with Rajendra Prasad as Principal
  • Gandhi inaugurated Bihar National College (5 Jan 1921) and Bihar Vidyapeeth (6 Feb 1921)
  • Mazhar-ul-Haq started 'The Motherland' newspaper (30 Sep 1921) for Hindu-Muslim unity
  • Movement called off 1922 after Chauri-Chaura incident

Civil Disobedience + Salt Satyagraha in Bihar

1930
  • Salt Satyagraha started 6 April 1930 in Bihar — from Champaran and Saran districts
  • Patna Salt Satyagraha inaugurated: 16 April 1930
  • Nehru toured Bihar 31 Mar – 3 Apr 1930 to prepare
  • Champaran, Bhojpur, Purnea, Saran, Muzaffarpur = key districts
  • Refusal to pay Chaukidari tax = major element
  • Women actively participated — Chandravati Devi, Shailbala Roy
  • Rajendra Prasad led Bihar Salt Satyagraha movement

⭐ Quit India Movement in Bihar

1942
  • Bihar Congress prepared outline under Rajendra Prasad on 31 July 1942
  • Congress banned 9 August 1942. Newspapers banned. All senior leaders arrested.
  • 11 August 1942: 7 students shot dead at Patna Legislative Assembly when they tried to hoist national flag. Collector WG Archer ordered open fire.
  • Names of martyrs: among them were Devipad Choudhary (commemorated by renamed school)
  • JP Narayan, Ram Manohar Lohia, Aruna Asaf Ali took charge when senior leaders arrested
  • 1943: JP Narayan organised Azad Dasta (Free Army)
  • Complete strike in Patna 12 August 1942 following the shootings

Bihar Socialist Party & First Government

1931–1937
  • Bihar Socialist Party founded 1931 by Ganga Sharan Sinha, Rambriksha Benipuri, Ramanand Mishra
  • Congress Socialist Party formally inaugurated 1934 by Jayaprakash Narayan at Anjuman Islamia Hall, Patna. Acharya Narendra Dev = President; JP = General Secretary
  • 1937 elections: Congress won majority (98/107 seats contested). Shri Krishna Singh declined to form government (no independent power)
  • Mohammad Yunus = first Prime Minister of Bihar (1937) as head of second-largest party
  • Yunus government lasted till 20 July 1937
  • Shri Krishna Singh formed Congress Cabinet 20 July 1937 — first Indian-led ministry

📝PYQ Practice
BPSC 2018
Who had invited Gandhiji to West Champaran district in Bihar?
(a) Rajkumar Shukla
(b) Mahadeo Desai
(c) Rajendra Prasad
(d) Narhari Parikh
✓ CORRECT: (a) Rajkumar Shukla

Rajkumar Shukla, a resident of Murli Bhitiharwa village, West Champaran, invited Gandhi at the 1916 Lucknow Congress session and later urged him repeatedly to visit Champaran. Gandhi arrived on 10 April 1917 and stayed at Sant Raut's house in Amolwa village. Option (c) Rajendra Prasad joined Gandhi on 15 April — he didn't invite Gandhi, he came to support him.

BPSC 2018
The first Indian Ministry in Bihar in 1937 came into existence under whose leadership?
(a) Shri Krishna Singh
(b) Mohammad Yunus
(c) GS Lal
(d) Wahab Ali
✓ CORRECT: (b) Mohammad Yunus

⚠️ Common Trap: Many answer Shri Krishna Singh. But the FIRST ministry in Bihar (1937) was under Mohammad Yunus — because Shri Krishna Singh (head of Congress, the majority party) declined to form government as he was not given independent powers. Yunus (head of second-largest party) was then invited. His government lasted till 20 July 1937, after which Shri Krishna Singh formed the Congress Cabinet.

BPSC MCQ
Bihar Socialist Party was formally inaugurated by:
(a) Jayaprakash Narayan
(b) MN Roy
(c) Subhash Chandra Bose
(d) Satya Bhagat
✓ CORRECT: (a) Jayaprakash Narayan

The Bihar Congress Socialist Party was formally inaugurated in 1934 by Jayaprakash Narayan at Anjuman Islamia Hall, Patna. JP became its General Secretary; Acharya Narendra Dev became President. Note: Bihar Socialist Party was FOUNDED in 1931 by Ganga Sharan Sinha, Rambriksha Benipuri, Ramanand Mishra — but formally inaugurated as the Congress Socialist Party in 1934 by JP.

✅Concept Check
1. When was the Tinkathia system abolished in Champaran?
✓ Correct! The Tinkathia system was abolished on 4 March 1918 as per the recommendations of the Champaran Agrarian Committee (constituted 10 June 1917). Option (a) 10 April 1917 = Gandhi's arrival date. Option (d) 1 May 1918 = Champaran Agrarian Act passed. Know all these dates separately.
✗ Answer is (C) 4 March 1918. Tinkathia system abolished per Champaran Agrarian Committee recommendation. Timeline: Gandhi arrived 10 Apr 1917 → Committee formed 10 Jun 1917 → Tinkathia abolished 4 Mar 1918 → Agrarian Act passed 1 May 1918.
2. Who was the leader of the first Congress Ministry in Bihar (which came after Mohammad Yunus's government)?
✓ Correct! Shri Krishna Singh (Bihar Kesari) formed the first Congress Cabinet on 20 July 1937. After Mohammad Yunus's government (first ministry, 1937) fell, Shri Krishna Singh — who had earlier declined to form government — formed the Congress Cabinet. Other cabinet members: Narayan Singh, Dr Syed Mehmood, Jaglal Choudhary, etc.
✗ Answer is (A) Shri Krishna Singh. He led the first Congress Ministry on 20 July 1937. Note the order: (1) Congress wins election → (2) Shri Krishna Singh declines → (3) Mohammad Yunus forms ministry (1st Bihar PM) → (4) Yunus falls 20 Jul 1937 → (5) Shri Krishna Singh forms Congress Cabinet.
MAINS GS-I · 150 WORDS
Champaran Satyagraha (1917) is called the first civil disobedience movement in India. Critically analyse its significance for Bihar and the national freedom movement.
Introduction

The Champaran Satyagraha (1917) was a watershed moment in Indian history — Gandhi's first experiment with civil disobedience in India, and Bihar's first successful mass movement against colonial economic exploitation.

Background
  • Tinkathia system: European planters forced peasants to grow indigo on 3/20 (tinkath = 3/20) of their land, with high rents and illegal levies
  • Rajkumar Shukla, a semi-literate Champaran peasant, tracked Gandhi to the 1916 Lucknow Congress session and persuaded him to come
  • Gandhi arrived 10 April 1917 — defied magistrate's expulsion order (first act of Indian civil disobedience)
Significance
  • For Bihar: Tinkathia system abolished 4 March 1918 — direct economic relief to lakhs of peasants; Bihar Tenancy law reformed
  • For Gandhi: Proved that Satyagraha worked in India (had earlier worked in South Africa); transformed him from South Africa's lawyer to India's leader
  • For national movement: Bihar leaders (Rajendra Prasad, Anugrah Narayan, Mazharul Haque, JB Kriplani) trained in Champaran became pillars of future national movements
  • For peasants: First time a national leader engaged directly with agrarian exploitation — established the model of peasant-national movement linkage
Conclusion

Champaran was more than an agrarian reform — it was Bihar's gift to Indian nationalism. The boy from Murli Bhitiharwa (Rajkumar Shukla) who brought Gandhi here triggered a chain of events that led, eventually, to India's independence.
