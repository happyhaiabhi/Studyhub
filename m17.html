MODULE 17·Modern Bihar

Revolt of 1857
in Bihar

Bihar's 1857 revolt was among the most intense in India. An 80-year-old Rajput king from Jagdishpur, Babu Kunwar Singh, became its greatest hero — fighting British forces across Bihar, UP, and back again, winning battles even as he lay dying from a battle wound.

BPSC Special Mains GS-I ⚠ Trap: Bihar revolt started 12 June 1857 at Rohini (Deoghar). Patna revolt = 3 July 1857 under Pir Ali. Kunwar Singh died 26 April 1858. Munger remained UNAFFECTED.
🎭Story HookSTART HERE

The year is 23 April 1858. On the banks of the Ganga near Shivpur Ghat, an 80-year-old man is leading his soldiers across the river. A British soldier shoots him in the wrist. The arm is nearly severed. What does Babu Kunwar Singh do?

He draws his sword with his other hand, cuts off his own wounded arm, and throws it into the Ganges as an offering to the river. Then he continues leading his troops. Three days later, on 26 April 1858, he returns victorious to his homeland of Jagdishpur — and then, finally, allows himself to die of his wounds.

This is the spirit of Bihar's 1857 revolt. It began on 12 June 1857 at Rohini (Deoghar) — not at Meerut — and it burned through Arrah, Patna, Tirhut, Saran, Gaya, and Hazaribagh. Old men, young soldiers, booksellers, and kings — all of Bihar rose against the Company.

💡 Babu Kunwar Singh, born 1777 — was 80 years old when he led one of India's most audacious revolts. Age was no barrier when the cause was freedom.

⭐ Babu Kunwar Singh — Bihar's Greatest 1857 Hero

Born 1777 · Died 26 April 1858 · Age 80 at revolt

Born 1777 at Jagdishpur, Bhojpur district (then Shahabad). Belonged to Ujjaini Rajput royal family.

  • 27 July 1857: Occupied Arrah (Shahabad district). Defeated British forces alongside Nana Saheb at Azamgarh.
  • Rebels from Danapur, Chota Nagpur, Manbhum, Singhbhum and Palamu joined his leadership.
  • Had manufacturing unit of arms at Jagdishpur. Fort stocked to feed 20,000 men for 6 months.
  • Supporters: brother Amar Singh, nephew Rathbhanjan Singh, Hari Kishan Singh. King of Dumrao = ally.
  • August 1857: Vincent Eyer captured Jagdishpur, destroyed fort. Kunwar Singh continued fighting across Bihar and UP.
  • 23 April 1858: Defeated British force under Captain Le Grand. Shot in wrist; cut off own arm; offered it to Ganga.
  • 26 April 1858: Returned victorious to Jagdishpur. Died of wounds.
  • Fight continued by brother Amar Singh and nephew Hari Kishan Singh till revolt suppressed (December 1859).
Patna Division

Deeply affected. Commissioner = William Tayler. Pir Ali led Patna revolt (3 July). Dr Lyell killed. Wahabi leaders also active. Gaya — villagers formed rebel groups.

Shahabad (Arrah)

Kunwar Singh occupied Arrah 27 July 1857. Jeodhar Singh and Haider Ali Khan = rebel leaders. Most intense region outside Patna.

Muzaffarpur & Tirhut

25 July 1857 — army revolted, killed Englishmen. Revolt in Champaran and Saran due to 12th Irregular Cavalry mutiny at Sugauli.

Hazaribagh

Infantry revolted July 1857. Leader: Madhav Singh. Vishwanath Shahdeo, Pandey Ganpat Rai = other leaders; captured and sentenced to death.

Singhbhum & Palamu

Raja Arjun Singh of Porhat revolted. Nilamber and Pitamber = Palamu leaders. Tribal people joined alongside soldiers.

Danapur Cantt

25 July 1857 — Danapur Cantt revolted. Starting point of widespread Bihar revolt. Soldiers plundered treasury; proclaimed Kunwar Singh as leader.

Maharajas Who Helped British

Maharajas of Darbhanga, Dumraon, Hatwa helped the British. Munger remained unaffected by the revolt.

Outcome

Bengal Tenancy Act 1885 — defined rights of tenants in Bihar and Bengal. Direct result of 1857 discontent against zamindars.

Babu Kunwar Singh belonged to the royal Rajput family of Jagdishpur, which is currently part of Bhojpur district of Bihar. Born 1777, he was about 80 years old when he led the revolt. Jagdishpur was also where he returned and died on 26 April 1858 after his last victorious battle.

(b) 17th June, 1858
(c) 26th April, 1858

✅Concept Check
1. Who led the Revolt of 1857 in Patna on 3 July 1857?
✓ Correct! Pir Ali Khan, a bookseller, led the Patna revolt on 3 July 1857. The Deputy Opium Agent Dr Lyell was killed by the mob. Pir Ali, Luft Ali Khan, Shaikh Ghasila, rebel sepoy Mahabat Ali, and a police Jamadar named Ali were all hanged. Kunwar Singh led the Jagdishpur/Arrah revolt, not the Patna city revolt.
✗ Answer is (A) Pir Ali Khan. He was a bookseller (NOT a king or soldier) who led the Patna revolt on 3 July 1857. This is a BPSC-tested fact — the combination of his profession (bookseller) and leadership role makes him distinctive.
2. Which part of Bihar remained UNAFFECTED by the Revolt of 1857?
✓ Correct! Munger remained UNAFFECTED by the Revolt of 1857. This is a standard BPSC trap question — while Patna, Arrah, Danapur, Muzaffarpur, Tirhut, Hazaribagh all saw intense revolts, Munger was peaceful. Also remember: Maharajas of Darbhanga, Dumraon, and Hatwa actually helped the British.
✗ Answer is (D) Munger. It was the only major Bihar region that remained unaffected. Arrah (Kunwar Singh), Danapur (sepoy mutiny 25 July), and Muzaffarpur (25 July army revolt) were all significantly affected.
MAINS GS-I · 150 WORDS
Discuss the role of Babu Kunwar Singh in the Revolt of 1857 in Bihar. Why is he considered the greatest hero of Bihar's 1857 revolt?
Introduction

Babu Kunwar Singh (1777–1858) of Jagdishpur, Bhojpur, stands as the most iconic figure of Bihar's 1857 revolt — an 80-year-old Rajput king who turned military reverses into moral victories and refused to surrender until his dying breath.

Military Leadership
  • 27 July 1857: Occupied Arrah (Shahabad district) — the most significant early military victory of Bihar's revolt
  • Led forces from Bihar to Awadh, fighting alongside Nana Saheb at Azamgarh
  • Built a remarkably broad coalition — rebels from Danapur, Chota Nagpur, Manbhum, Singhbhum, Palamu
  • Maintained fighting capability even after Vincent Eyer destroyed his Jagdishpur fort (August 1857)
The Legendary Final Battle

On 23 April 1858, at Shivpur Ghat, Kunwar Singh defeated Captain Le Grand's forces. When shot in the wrist, he cut off his own arm and offered it to the Ganga — a gesture that has become legendary. He returned to Jagdishpur victoriously on 26 April 1858, and died the same day of his wounds.

Conclusion

Kunwar Singh's greatest contribution was transforming a military revolt into a symbol of Bihar's resistance. At 80, defying physical limitations, leading diverse forces — he demonstrated that freedom's call knows no age. His legacy is celebrated every year on Bihar Diwas, and Jagdishpur proudly bears his memory.
