MODULE 11·Medieval Bihar

Khilji, Tughlaq &
Noohani Dynasties

Three dynasties across 230 years — Bihar passed from Khilji governors to Tughlaq control to Afghan independence under the Noohanis. This module covers Bihar's transition through the Delhi Sultanate, the naming of Bihar as a province, and the rise of the Noohanis whose fall set the stage for Sher Shah Suri.

BPSC Prelims Mains GS-I ⚠ Trap: "Bihar" named as province during Tughlaq era · Darbhanga = Tughlaqpur · Noohani dynasty = BPSC Special
🎭Story HookSTART HERE

The year is 1324 AD. Sultan Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq's army has just routed the last Karnata king of Mithila. As the sultan rides through the newly conquered lands of Tirhut, his scribes are busy — minting new coins, inscribing mosque walls, renaming everything. The ancient town of Darbhanga gets a new identity: "Tughlaqpur."

A 1326 AD Arabic inscription on the wall of Darbhanga's Jama Masjid reads: "Iqlim Tughluqpur urf Tirhut" — the province of Tughlaqpur, also known as Tirhut. This is the moment Bihar formally became a named, administered Sultanate province.

Two centuries later, it would be a young boy playing in the lanes near Sasaram — the son of a horse-keeper — who would avenge every humiliation Bihar suffered. His name? Farid Khan. The world would call him Sher Shah Suri.

💡 Bihar's name origin: "Bihar" province = named after town of Bihar Sharif = named after Buddhist Viharas = ultimately named after Sanskrit "Vihara" (monastery). A Buddhist word became a province's identity under Muslim rule — the perfect irony of history.
🧠Core Concept — Three Dynasties in BiharWITH ANALOGY
🏛️ Modern Analogy

Think of Bihar during 1290–1526 AD as a contested frontier state in a weak federal system. Delhi (the centre) kept changing — Khilji, Tughlaq, Lodhi — each weakening further. Bihar's governors were like CMs who sometimes obeyed Delhi, sometimes ignored it. The Noohanis are like a Bihar CM who declared full independence when Delhi was too distracted by its own civil wars. Sher Shah (next module) is the one who finally ran away with the whole country.

1. Khilji Dynasty in Bihar (1290–1320 AD)

Limited control · S. Bihar only
Start 1290 AD — Jalaluddin Khalji became Sultan. Khalji dynasty begins in Delhi.
Alauddin 1296 AD — Alauddin Khalji ascended. Demanded half the crops (50%) on land after measurement as revenue. Sent Sheikh Mohammad Ismail to Darbhanga — Raja Sakra Singh of Karnata dynasty defeated him.
Control Level Limited — Khalji coins found only at Bhojpur and Lakhisarai. North Bihar (Karnata/Tirhut rulers) still semi-independent. Bihar was a contested buffer zone.
Bengal Connection 1309–1321 AD — Sultan Shamsuddin Firozshah (Bengal) declared himself ruler; appointed son Hatim Khan as Governor of Bihar.

2. Tughlaq Dynasty in Bihar (1324–1398 AD)

⭐ Most BPSC-tested era for Bihar
Ghiyasuddin 1324 AD — Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq invaded Bengal and Bihar. Defeated Harisimhadeva (last Karnata king of Mithila) — Karnata dynasty ended. Established mint at Tirhut → called "Tughlaqabad-urf-Tirhut" (Darbhanga). Tughlaq coins found from Tirhut.
Bihar Named ⚠ "Bihar" got its official name as a province during the Tughlaq era — NOT Slave or Khilji era. 1326 AD Arabic inscription on Jama Masjid wall, Darbhanga reads: "Iqlim Tughluqpur urf Tirhut." Relates to Muhammad bin Tughlaq. Bihar Sharif = capital of Bihar province.
Muh. bin Tughlaq Son of Ghiyasuddin. Darbhanga = Tughlaqpur in his time. Fort and Jama Masjid built here. Sukunat inscription at Bihar Sharif confirms his control. Malik Baya (Ibrahim Baya) appointed — his tomb is at Bihar Sharif (Pir Pahari hill). Sufi Saint Sharfuddin Yahya Maneri's writings illuminate this era.
Firoz Tughlaq Established Bihar as a separate province detached from Bengal. Reinstated Kameshwar Thakur — founder of Oiniwar dynasty in Mithila. Oiniwar dynasty governed N. Bihar (Mithila) from 1353–1526 AD. Persian inscription at Bihar Sharif from his reign.
End of Tughlaq 1398–99 AD — Taimur's invasion of Delhi effectively ended Tughlaq power. Bihar passed to Sharqi (Jaunpur) rule. Sultan Ibrahim Shah Sharqi built mosque in Darbhanga.

3. Sharqi Rule + Noohani Dynasty (1394–1529 AD)

⭐ BPSC Special — Noohani = Bihar's own Afghan dynasty
Sharqi Period 1394 AD — Malik Sarwar Khwaja Jahan sent to rule from Kanauj to Bihar. Sharqis of Jaunpur controlled Tirhut and South Bihar. Sultan Ibrahim Shah Sharqi built mosque in Darbhanga. Last Sharqi king fell to Sikandar Lodhi.
Dariya Khan Dariya Khan Noohani — appointed administrator of Bihar by Sikandar Lodhi (after defeating Hussain Shah Sharqi). Administered Bihar till his death in 1523. Son Bahar Khan Noohani succeeded him.
Sultan Mohammad ⚠ Bahar Khan Noohani (1523 AD) declared independence and took title "Sultan Mohammad." Defeated Ibrahim Lodhi initially. Extended empire from Bihar to Kannauj. Very important for BPSC — "first PM of Bihar" Mohd. Yunus was named after this tradition.
Battle of Ghaghra 1529 AD — Sultan Mohammad defeated by Babur in Battle of Ghaghra (Gogra). Accepted Babur's authority. After Sultan Mohammad's death, son Jalal Khan succeeded. Sher Shah Suri was appointed advisor/protector of Jalal Khan. After Noohanis fell → Sher Shah emerged as powerful Afghan ruler.
Significance Noohani dynasty = last independent pre-Mughal Afghan power in Bihar. Their fall directly enabled Sher Shah Suri's rise. Tuzuk-i-Babari (Babur's memoir) gives account of fall of Noohanis.

BPSC PrelimsMCQ Bank
Bihar name was given to this place in the reign of which kingdom?
(a) Slave
(b) Khilji
(c) Tughlaq
(d) Noohani
✓ CORRECT: (c) Tughlaq

Bihar got its official name as a province during the Tughlaq dynasty era. A 1326 AD Arabic inscription on the Jama Mosque wall in Darbhanga reads: "Iqlim Tughluqpur urf Tirhut" — confirming Tughlaq control and provincial naming. Under Muhammad bin Tughlaq, Bihar Sharif served as the capital of the Bihar province. Before this, the region was known by various geographic names but not formally called "Bihar" as a province.

Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq invaded Bengal and Bihar in:
(a) 1324 AD
(b) 1320 AD
(c) 1319 AD
(d) 1318 AD
✓ CORRECT: (a) 1324 AD

Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq invaded Bengal and Bihar in 1324 AD. He defeated the Lakhnauti king Nasiruddin who surrendered, and defeated Harisimhadeva of the Karnata dynasty in Mithila. After this conquest, Ghiyasuddin established the Tughlaq presence in Bihar and created the mint town "Tughlaqabad-urf-Tirhut" (Darbhanga).

BPSC Prelims67th BPSC / MCQ Bank
Who was a very important dynasty in the history of Bihar (pre-Mughal Afghan dynasty)?
(a) Tughlaq dynasty
(b) Noohani dynasty
(c) Khilji dynasty
(d) None of the above
✓ CORRECT: (b) Noohani dynasty

The Noohani dynasty is specifically called "a very important dynasty in the history of Bihar" in sources (Arihant Bihar History). Founded effectively by Dariya Khan Noohani (appointed by Sikandar Lodhi), it became independent under Bahar Khan (Sultan Mohammad) in 1523 AD, extending from Bihar to Kannauj. Its fall directly created the power vacuum that allowed Sher Shah Suri to rise. The Tuzuk-i-Babari (Babur's memoir) provides the key account of this dynasty's end.

✅Concept CheckTEST YOURSELF
1. Who appointed Dariya Khan Noohani as the administrator of Bihar?
✓ Correct! Sikandar Lodhi (of the Lodi dynasty) defeated Hussain Shah Sharqi (last Sharqi ruler) and appointed Dariya Khan Noohani as administrator of Bihar. Dariya Khan remained Bihar's administrator till his death in 1523 AD. This is a key BPSC fact — students often confuse it with Tughlaq-era appointments.
✗ Answer is (C) Sikandar Lodhi. He defeated the last Sharqi ruler and appointed Dariya Khan Noohani as Bihar's administrator. The Noohani dynasty came from the Lodi-era political rearrangements — NOT from Tughlaq-era appointments.
2. Babur fought Battle of Ghaghra (1529) with whom?
✓ Correct! Babur fought the Battle of Ghaghra (Gogra) in 1529 against Sultan Mohammad (Bahar Khan Noohani). Sultan Mohammad accepted Babur's authority after defeat. After his death, his son Jalal Khan succeeded, and Sher Shah was appointed Jalal Khan's protector — which became Sher Shah's political launchpad.
✗ Answer is (A) Sultan Mohammad (Bahar Khan Noohani). He was the Noohani king who had declared independence as "Sultan Mohammad" in 1523. Babur defeated him at the Battle of Ghaghra (1529). Sher Shah rose to power only after the Noohani decline — he was not yet powerful enough to fight Babur.

Introduction

From Bakhtiyar Khalji's invasion (1197 AD) to Babur's victory (1526 AD), Bihar experienced 330 years of Delhi Sultanate control, marked by shifting loyalties, incomplete annexation, and ultimately, Afghan independence.

Phase-wise Evolution
  • Slave Dynasty (1225–90): Iltutmish formally annexed Bihar; Bihar Sharif became provincial capital
  • Khilji Dynasty (1290–1320): Limited control (coins only in south Bihar); Karnata-ruled Tirhut remained semi-independent
  • Tughlaq Dynasty (1324–98): Most transformative — Ghiyasuddin conquered Bihar; Bihar officially named as province; Firoz separated it from Bengal; Oiniwar dynasty established in Mithila
  • Sharqi Rule (1394–1510s): Post-Taimur Jaunpur Sultanate controlled Bihar
  • Noohani Dynasty (1519–29): Dariya Khan Noohani (Sikandar Lodhi's appointee) → Bahar Khan declared independence as "Sultan Mohammad" → extended to Kannauj → defeated by Babur at Ghaghra (1529)
Noohani as Transitional Force

The Noohanis' collapse created the perfect power vacuum: their last ruler Jalal Khan appointed Sher Shah Suri as his protector. This gave Sher Shah the institutional foothold, military base, and political legitimacy to eventually overthrow the Mughals entirely — making the Noohani dynasty not a footnote but a crucial hinge in Bihar and Indian history.

Conclusion

Bihar's 330 years under the Sultanate ended with an Afghan from Sasaram poised to redirect the course of Indian history — a fitting conclusion to a period when the region had absorbed conquest after conquest, yet retained the human capital that would produce India's most efficient pre-Mughal administrator.
